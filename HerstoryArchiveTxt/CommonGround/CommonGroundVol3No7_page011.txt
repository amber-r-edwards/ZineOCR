Q  

I loved your mother, village wise woman, before you.  
Her shreds of nourishment arrive with you.  
What luck that she in her wisdom,  

honed keen to the bone,  
tucked-in severity,  

Gave you what little flesh she could.  

She brought you the last step from village to town.  
What luck the bones in their wisdom  

bore you out beyond the trees to watch me leave,  

to make my journey into the hills.  

When I meet your mother on the path,  

I will comb her hair smooth  
and she will not know me.  

Barbara Phillips

The Gleaner  

John, the wind has shifted  
west to north  

these flakes are working up  
a storm  

she says, and pulling on  
a sweater ravelled  

by the years  

and field-mice  

hurries out to  

strip the garden  

before dark  

kneeling  

fills her apron  

with winter squash  
and onions  

feeling summer  

slip through fingers  
with the soil.  

Warm, keep warm

the coldness  

feeling in her bones
