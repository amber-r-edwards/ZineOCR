JUNE IS GAY [AND LESBIAN] PRIDE MONTH and Buffalo's celebration, June 16-30, will feature a dance, workshops, a bicycle tour, films, and a non-denominational church service.

Among the workshops (described on the calendar page of this issue) are aging, fat politics, alternative parenting, relationships, single life, and coming out to parents.

The Gay [and Lesbian] Pride Fest calendar was not final at press time for Common Ground, but we include a partial list of the activities here. For further information, contact the Gay and Lesbian Community Center at 886-1274.

ROOM OF OUR OWN is the anthology of women's poetry published yearly by the Women's Writing Workshop of Women's Studies College at SUNY Buffalo. This 1985 issue, Volume #8, is a perfect bound, 80-page book that is the most geographically eclectic ROOM OF OUR OWN yet published. (Previous volumes have been saddle-stitched and have been more “Buffalo-centric". They have had no more than 64 pages.) Volume #8 also features the work of New York artist Julie Fein.

OLDER WORKERS PROGRAM: If you are a man or woman, 55 years or older, actively seeking employment, a resident of Erie County, outside the City of Buffalo, Cheektowaga and the Town of Tonawanda, and meet eligibility requirements of the Private Industry Council, give us a call at 847-1120. The program provides Job Readiness Training, career/job counseling, job placement assistance, job club, and individual counseling. Participants will be given information and assistance in career planning, job search techniques, assertiveness training, resume writing, interviewing, and personal development. Call the Center at 847-1120 for information, eligibility requirements, and to arrange an appointment. This program is administered by Everywoman Opportunity Center and funded by a Job Training Partnership Act grant from Erie County Partnership.
