WHO ARE THE WOMEN IN COMMON GROUND? Common Ground, which is open to any woman who wants to do the

work of putting out the paper, started as the idea of one woman who encouraged four friends to work on it with her. These five women began a community survey and outreach to discover what kind of paper women would support. Over 120 individual women responded with ideas, offers of help, and encouragement. Groups and organizations also responded, and CG now has a network of over 60 groups we communicate with. We held two open community meetings at which the 25 or so women who have joined were recruited. 

The women of Common Ground are a diverse group and hope to maintain and broaden their membership. We are between 19 and 51 years of age, with a median age of about 30. We are single, married, and divorced. Several of us have children, are grandmothers, or choose to remain childless. We have chosen very traditional lifestyles and very unconventional lifestyles. We live from Newfane to Bolivar, in Buffalo and the suburbs. We are working class and middle class, and have educational attainments ranging from G.E.D.s to PhDs.
