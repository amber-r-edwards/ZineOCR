JUST BUFFALO will offer residencies to four emerging WNY writers in 1985 in exchange for community service projects of their own design. In order to qualify, applicants must have been residents of WNY for nine of the twelve months prior to application. Previous winners of this competition are ineligible for three years following the end of their prior award period.

Interested writers must submit ten poems or fifteen pages of prose in 5 copy sets for blind judging by a panel of five respected individuals from WNY's literary community. Applicants can submit in both categories, but can only win in one.

Submissions must have the writer's name, address, phone number, and titles on a top sheet or they will not be returned. Incorrectly marked submissions will not be considered for evaluation. Submissions must be in the JUST BUFFALO OFFICE at the Allentown Community Center, 211 Elmwood Ave.; Buffalo, 14201 by or before December 10th.

Announcement of the winners and of the panel of judges will be made in the January issue of COMMON GROUND. For more information, call JUST BUFFALO at the Allentown Community Center: 885-6400.

FEMINIST COLLECTIVE MEMBERS, past and present, are invited to participate in a national survey on women's experiences in feminist collectives. Though many women have been involved in feminist collectives during the last 10-15 years, there is very little written about women's experiences in these groups. Research is being conducted by a former collective member interested in writing about the particular joys and struggles which are common to organizations without hierarchies. Please contact Maureen Hicks, 1355 Westwood, #207, Los Angeles, CA 90024, or call (213) 820-2515 for more information.

TELEPHONE A FRIEND is an organized volunteer service which calls elderly, handicapped, chronically or temporarily ill persons who live alone. Its basic purpose is to help satisfy the desire of people to live independently by eliminating some of the fears and dangers that living alone involves. Regular telephone calls are made at a scheduled time, agreed upon by the caller and the person being called. For further information on receiving calls or becoming a volunteer caller, contact 887-2625.
