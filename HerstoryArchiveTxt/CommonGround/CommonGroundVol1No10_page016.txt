JUNE 1983

This month marks the fourteenth
year that Lesbian and Gay Pride
Celebrations have been held through-
out this country! It is the anni-
versary of the "Stonewall Riots" of
June 28, 1969, when police on a
“routine” raid of a N.Y.C. gay bar,
found that this time the customers
fought back, a week of
open resistance by Gays in the
streets of N.Y.C.

Buffalo is celebrating two and a
half weeks of Gay Pride Fest Activi-
ties this year, beginning on June 1,
with a discussion and wine and cheese
party with Virginia Apuzzo, Execu-
tive director of the National Gay
Task Force. This event, sponsored
by the Mattachine Society of the
Niagara Frontier, will be at 7:30
pm, at the Unitarian Church at Elm-
wood and Ferry. Admission is $3,
with a 50¢ discount to Mattachine
members. (Proceeds beyond expenses
to benefit Fifth Freedom and the
Gay Hotline.)

June 3 is the Ms. Buffalo Cruise,
a popular annual event. Boarding is
at 7 pm at the Naval and Servicemen's
Park. $7 tickets for this event are
available at City Lights, Crossroads,
EMMA, Gay and Lesbian Community Cen-
ter, M.C. Comptons and Rhinopotamus.

A worship "Service of Liberation"
will be conducted at 6:30 pm on Sun-
day, June 5, co-sponsored by the
Metropolitan Community Church and
Dignity, at the Unitarian Church of
Amherst. Call 689-0890 for car pool
information.

"The AIDS Epidemic: What You Need
To Know" will be the title of a pre-
sentation by Poss Hewitt, M.D., to
be held Monday, June 6, at the Nia-
gara Porter Library. Dr. Hewitt has
had direct experience working with
AIDS patients, and his slide presen-
tation is designed for the gay com-
munity at large, - 9 p.m.

For those concerned with recon-

ciling their Catholic or religious

faith with a lesbian or gay lifestyle
there will be a workshop presented

Wednesday, June 8, 7 pm at the Gay
and Lesbian Community Center. This
will be given by Dignity/Buffalo, an
organization serving the gay and
Catholic community--for further in-
formation, contact Dignity, 874-4139.
"Criminal Law and the Gay Life-
style" will be the subject of a
workshop presented by William Gard-
ner, a Buffalo attorney well known
throughout the gay community. It
will be held on Friday, June 10 at
8 pm at the Unitarian Church of
Buffalo, and will examine the crim-
inal laws as they are used and mis-
used against gay persons. An infor-
mal wine and cheese party will
follow.

On Saturday, June 11 at 7:30 pm,
at the Gay and Lesbian Community

Center, there will be a poetry
reading and discussion, including a
brief presentation examining a his-
torical perspective on gay poetry.
Anyone interested in presenting
their poetry should leave a message

at G.L.C.C.

The Annual Gay Pride Fest Picnic
will be at Chestnut Ridge State Park,
shelter 37 on Sunday June 12 from
noon until dusk. Bring your own
food--charcoal provided, beer and
iced tea sold, one dollar donation
requested.

"Issues in Gay Parenting and
Donor Insemination" is the title of
a workshop to be presented by CARASA
(Coalition for Abortion Rights and
Against Sterilization Abuse) on

Monday June 13, 7-9 pm at the

Niagara-Porter Library.

On Tuesday, June 14, 7 pm at

G.L.C.C., members of EMMA, The
Buffalo Women's Bookstore Collective
will sponsor a workshop titled
“Themes in Women's Music: How It
Speaks To Women". The workshop will
examine various themes such as sex-
uality, mothers, etc. through the
recorded songs of notable and lesser
known women musicians, discussion

is encouraged. (Women only.)

This month is also the first
anniversary of Relatives and Friends
of Gays, a support group for non-
gays who have lesbian and gay rela-
tives and loved ones. Their cele-
bration will be Wednesday, June 15
from 7-8:30 pm at Child and Family
Services, 330 Delaware Ave. (entrance
off Tracy St., ring bell). All les-
bian and gay persons, as well as
their relatives and friends are invi-
ted. For further information, call
Tom Hammond, 842-2750 (days) or
885-0267 (evenings).

The Metropolitan Community Church
of Buffalo, serving the Lesbian &
Gay Community, is sponsoring a four
week course on "Homosexuality and
the Bible", which will culminate in
a debate to be held June 16, 7 pm
at the Niagara-Porter Library.

The final event of Gay Pride Fest
is the Women's Dance at the Unitarian
Church of Buffalo, 9-1 am. $2 admis-
sion, refreshments sold. This
is the first of this year's events
sponsored by the Women's Dance/Event
Coalition, to be held Friday, June 17.

Further information on most of
these events can be obtained by
calling the Gay and Lesbian Community
Center, 886-1274.
