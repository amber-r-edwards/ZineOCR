Help is available for Displaced Homemakers. A one of a kind holiday celebration can be found December 10, 1988, 10 am to 5 pm at Lexington Corners (the intersection of Lexington and Ashland Avenues) in Buffalo, N.Y. Now in its 7th year, the annual conjunction of artists and craftspeople will be mixed with all the food, music and fun of the holiday season. Activities for all ages and varied interests are planned.  

The Network of Light gallery and the Yeast West Bakery will host the Gifts from Hands Craft Show, beautiful handcrafts by local artisans. The Lexington Food Cooperative will add to its special holiday foods, calendars, kitchenware and books, a poinsettia and potted Christmas tree sale.  

Circles bookstore will have palm readings, a workshop on chants and magical songs, and an herb magic video. In the evening at 8 pm the Network of Light at 224 Lexington Avenue offers “Probably Still Primitive", American and British Isles folk and contemporary music. The $4 donation will benefit Network of Light.
