at the North Area YWCA, 2844 Dela-  
ware, Kenmore, each Wednesday from 7-9  
p.m. Open to all divorced and sep-  
arated women who have passed through  
the crisis stage of loss and are  
ready to move on to a positive  
approach to single life and personal  
growth. Includes some social events.

aid available.

beginning in Lancaster, northern

CONTRIBUTORS  

RUTH AVILA  
was born in Peru and has been in  
this country since the 1950's.  

JULIE ALANE SIMONS  
is 27 and was born in Salamanca, N.Y.  
She spent two years in South and  
Central America as a teacher and  
photographer. She also lived in  
Berkley, Ca. doing freelance illus-  
tration and photography. She now  
teaches art and music to emotion-  
ally disturbed children in Catta-  
raugus County.  

KAREN PETERSON*  
is a poet and writer who has per-  
formed her work locally, and been  
published in A ROOM OF OUR OWN.  

JUDY TRUPIN  
is a poet, dancer, and choreographer.  
Based in New York City, she is the  
founder of Extrapolating, a perfor-  
mance group that "treads the border-  
line between theatre and dance."  

JOY WALSH*  
is the author of Locating Positions,  
a book of poetry published by Street  
Press. She is the editor of Moody  
Street Irregulars: A Jack Kerouac  
Newsletter, and Textile. Bridge Press,  
which published poetry and fiction.  
She works with the American Humanist  
Society as advertising manager for  
The Humanist Magazine. Her work has  
been translated into Japanese and  
published in a journal entitled  
blue jacket. She has published in  
Europe and Australia as well as in  
the U.S.A.  

BARBARA HOCK*  
is an artist, poet and journalist.  
Her book of illustrated poems, “Rain  
Dancer" will be out in April.  

ANA HIDALGO  
was born in Chile, and has been liv-  
ing in Buffalo for 7 years. She  
works both in the community and the  
university as an artist & professor,  
trying to reach as far and as effect-  
ively as possible in all walks of life.  

DORIS SANTERCOLE  
is a native born Buffalonian, educat-  
ed at Villa Maria College and the  
University of Buffalo. She has been  
making and showing her photographs  
for the last five years.  

*Joy Walsh, Barbara Hock and Karen  
Peterson's work appeared in the Jan-  
uary issue of CG without their bio-  
graphical information.
