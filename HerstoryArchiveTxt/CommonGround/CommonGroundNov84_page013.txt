JOB READINESS TRAINING - If you have  
been a homemaker and now need help  
finding a job, you may qualify for  
Job Readiness Training through the  
Displaced Homemaker Center of WNY.  
A displaced homemaker is a person who  
has cared for home and family for  
many years and has lost the major  
source of income through death,  
divorce, separation, disability or  
loss of job by their spouse. Dis-  
placed Homemakers need training to  
upgrade or develop their skills and  
may need financial, medical, legal  
or personal counseling.

Programs are offered free of  
charge to Western New York residents.  
Workshop participants will be given  
information and assistance in career/  
life planning, resume writing, deci-  
sion making, job search skills, in-  
terviewing skills and techniques as  
well as legal advice, networking and  
other related topics.

Classes will begin for this set  
of workshops on November 13 and run  
through December 4, 1984. The three  
week workshop will be scheduled from  
9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Monday through  
Thursday. They will be held at the  
Maryvale Continuing/Community Educa-  
tion Building located at 777 Maryvale  
Drive near Beach Road. Conveniently  
located on a bus route, day care ser-  
vices will be available. For more  
information regarding pre-registration  
call 847-8850.

JUST BUFFALO RESIDENCIES - in an effort  
to recognize the achievements of  
WNY writers, and to assist in devel-  
oping new audiences for their works,  
JUST BUFFALO will offer residencies  
to four emerging WNY writers in 1985  
in exchange for community service  
projects of their own design.

In order to qualify, applicants  
must have been residents of WNY for  
nine of the twelve months prior to  
application. Previous winners of  
this competition are ineligible for  
three years following the end of  
their prior award period.

Interested writers must submit  
ten poems or fifteen pages of prose  
in 5 copy sets for blind judging by  
a panel of five respected individuals  
from WNY's literary community. Appli-  
cants can submit in both categories,  
but can only win in one.

Submissions must have the writer's  
name, address, phone number, poems  
titles (or first lines) and/or prose  
titles on a top sheet only. Manu-  
scripts must be accompanied by an  
SASE or they will not be returned.  
Incorrectly marked MSs will not be  
considered for evaluation. Submissions  
must be in the JUST BUFFALO OFFICE  
at the Allentown Community Center,  
111 Elmwood Ave., Buffalo, 14201 by  
or before December 15, 1984.

Announcement of selected writers,  
and of the panel of judges, will be  
made early in the Spring of 1985.

For more information, call JUST  
BUFFALO at the Allentown Community  
Center: 885-6400.

FEMINIST COLLECTIVE MEMBERS, past and  
present, are invited to participate in  
a national survey on women's experiences  
in feminist collectives. Though  
many women have been involved in  
feminist collectives during the last  
10-15 years, there is very little  
written about women's experiences in  
these groups. Research is being con-  
ducted by a former collective mem-  
ber interested in writing about the  
particular joys and struggles which  
are common to organizations without  
hierarchies. Please contact Maureen  
Hicks, 1355 Westwood, #207, Los  
Angeles, CA 90024, or call (213)  
820-2515 for more information.
