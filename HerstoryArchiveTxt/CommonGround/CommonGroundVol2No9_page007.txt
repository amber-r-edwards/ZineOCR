| Peace With Justice

“We must work like mad to make
sure that all the children of the
world survive. Nothing else really
matters." These are the words of
Dr. Helen Caldicott, founder of
Women's Action for Nuclear Disarma-
ment, and keynote speaker of
Peace With Justice Week. "Work
like mad" many local organizations
did, and there are a wide variety of
events being sponsored throughout the
week of May 4-12 in Western New York.

Dr. Helen Caldicott is also pres-
ident Emeritus of Physicians for
Social Responsibility and author of
books such as Nuclear Madness. Her
address "We The People: The Hope for
Ending the Arms Race" will be at 12
noon at Erie Community College, City
Campus. A donation of $3 is requested.

May 5th will see a formal send-
off for "Tools for Peace in Nicaragua”
at Maryknoll House, 71 Jewett Park-
way. Agricultural, medical and edu-
cational tools are being collected by
OXFAM American Supporters. For more
information, call 835-4073.

The Peace & Justice Parade and
Fair is becoming an annual event for
those who want to make a statement
about their feelings on peace. The
parade begins on Sunday, May 6, at
2:30 in Niagara Square in front of
City Hall, moving up Delaware to the
Red Cross Headquarters at Delaware
and Summer. Activities and booths
will be at the end of the parade,
with the fair taking place from 3 to
7 pm.

“Images of the Soviet Union" will
be a dialogue taking place at 7 pm
at the Ebenezer United Church of
Christ, 630 Main St., West Seneca
on May 6.

The Buffalo and Erie County
Public Library is sponsoring a series
of films throughout the week, en-
titled "For the Love of This Planet".
All films are at the Downtown Branch,
most begin at 12:10 pm and are fol-
lowed by discussion. Monday, May
7 is War Game, made in the 1960s by
Peter Hawkins, one of the first anti-
nuclear war films. Tuesday is Gods
of Metal, about hope and concerned
individuals taking action, produced
by Maryknoll Missioners in 1982.
Discussant will be Dr. Théodore Mills,
Professor of Sociology at SUNYAB.

The Last Epidemic depicts the medical
consequences of a nuclear war. It
will be shown on Wednesday, along
with War Without Winners, another
factual view, narrated by Paul New-
man. No Place to Hide is a look back
in time at how America was sold on
fallout shelters, narrated by Martin
Sheen. It will be shown on Thursday,
and followed by a discussion with
Rev. Peter LaValley. Friday's film
will be If You Love This Planet, a

1983 Academy Award winning documentary

featuring Dr. Helen Caldicott. Dis-
cussant will be Sister Joan Malone of
the Center for Justice. Saturday's
film begins at 11 am. The Little
Prince is an animated version of
Antoine St. Exupéry's classic.

Monday, May 7, at 3 pm, there will
be a walk from the soup kitchen at St.
Vincent de Paul, 1107 Main at Best
to the Federal Building, where there

will be a Public Action Demonstration,

"Act for Jobs, Peace and Justice".
Co-sponsored by the Center for Justice
and the WNY Peace Center, call 835-
4073 for more information.

At 7:30 Monday evening there will
be a discussion entitled "Buffalo:
Nuclear Free City" at the Unitarian
Universalist Church, 695 Elmwood
Ave. Reverend Rudi Gelsey will
present and lead the discussion on
what Buffalo can do to protect itself
against a nuclear war and join in
the efforts toward a nuclear free
America.

“Firebreak II" will be a War-Peace
Role-Playing Game at Buffalo State
College, Communications Center West
at 7:30 pm on Tuesday, May 8, spon-
sored by Ground Zero.

Also on Tuesday evening, Bishop

John Wilson, a Miskito Indian from

Nicaragua and minister in the Mora-
vian Church will give a first-hand
account of the struggle in his home-
land at St. Paul's and St. Mark's
UCC Church of the Shoreline, 200
Niagara Street.

The Educators for Social Respon-
sibility will sponsor a film festival
in the College Learning Lab at Buffalo
State College. This will include such
films as If You Love This Planet and
In the Nuclear Shadow. On Wednesday,
May 9, the films will be followed by
discussion, displays and refreshments.

Wednesday evening there will be
two other events. A benefit concert
by ARS Nova Chamber Orchestra at
Asbury-Delaware United Church at
Tupper and Delaware to benefit several
food pantries. Donations are re-
quested. There will also be a forum
on “Jobs, Peace and Freedom" at
Canisius College, sponsored by the
WNY Coalition of Conscience.

“Dimensions in Racial Justice" is

a one-day conference sponsored by

the YWCA and other organizations at
Buffalo State College. Call 852-6120
for further information.

The Rev. William Sloane Coffin,
Jr. will preach at the second annual
World Peace Celebration on May 10.
This ecumenical worship service will
be held at 8 pm at St. Paul's Cathe-
dral, Main and Cathedral Park in
Downtown Buffalo.

The Buffalo Philharmonic Orchestra
will salute WNY Peace With Justice
Week with a Beethoven Concert at
Kleinhan's Music Hall at 8 pm on
Saturday, May 12.

Other events are being sponsored
in the month of May by Women's Action
for Nuclear Disarmament. The Pentagon
Ribbon Project is a nationwide effort
inviting women, men and children to
embroider, weave, stitch or hook
their hopes for peace onto a piece of
sturdy fabric. Pieces will be sewn
together, and on August 6, 1985, the
40th anniversary of Hiroshima, the
ribbon will be tied around the Penta-
gon. WAND plans to have local pieces
sewn together by August 6, 1984.

Call them at 835-4073.

Friday, May 11 is a Fast for
Peace and Justice, where we are
invited to give up food and join in
prayer, proclaiming peace.

WAND's Mother's Day Project will
be repeated this year. There will
be a table at Eastern Hills Mall on
Saturday, May 12, distributing flowers
as a reminder of peace.
