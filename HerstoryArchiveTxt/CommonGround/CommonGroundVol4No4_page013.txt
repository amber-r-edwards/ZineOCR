"Being Single in a Coupled World”  
will be the focus of a personal  
growth workshop led by psychotherapist  
Kay Patterson on Wed., December  
11th, 7-9 pm at Southtowns  
Women's Resource Center, 177 Main  
Street, Hamburg. There is a registration  
fee of $5 per person and  
advance registration is necessary.  
Some scholarships are available  

and group size is limited, call  
648-3448 for information and  
registration. The aim of the  
workshop is to help participants  
learn lifelong skills for coping  
with aloneness by redirecting  

their focus on the positive elements  
of being single. Open to men and  
women who are single by choice,  

or as the result of separation,  
divorce or death of a partner; as  
well as those whose partners are  
frequently absent because of business,  
career, etc. and have experienced  
difficulty in coping  

with singleness.

FIRST COMPREHENSIVE CHILD CARE INFOR-  
MATION AND REFERRAL CENTER in Western  
New York will open at the Downtown  
YWCA, 190 Franklin Street in Buffalo  
in July. The Child Care Resource  

and Referral Center is a project of  
the Child Care Coalition of Western  
New York, but will function as a YWCA  
program.  

Three kinds of services will be  
offered: centralized information on  
child-care services in Erie County,  
support services and technical  
assistance for parents and providers  
(to improve the quality of care),  
and assistance to employers who are  
seeking child care information on  
behalf of their employees.  

The Child Care Coalition of Western  
New York was founded in 1983 in re-  
sponse to the growing concern about  
good quality child care. Opening  
the Child Care Coalition Resource and  
Referral Center is the first objective  
of the group for 1984-85.  

The program was developed by the  
coalition, a diverse group which  
includes leaders from education,  
social agencies serving children  
and their families, the United Way,  
business, city, county and state  
government, the PTA and industry.  
It is chaired by Dorothy Millard of  
the Day Care Association of Erie  
County.
