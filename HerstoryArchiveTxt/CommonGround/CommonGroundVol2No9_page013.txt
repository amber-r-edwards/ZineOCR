MAY 10 - RACIAL JUSTICE: FACETS OF  
RACISM IN OUR COMMUNITY. "The elim-  
ination of racism, wherever it  
exists, and by any means necessary.”  
That goal is the One Imperative of  
the YWCA of the USA, established in  
their national convention in 1970,  
(and renewed at every subsequent  
convention,) as their single, most  
important aim. The manifestations  
of racism, how to identify and  

cope with them, will be explored  

by the YWCA and seven other spon-  
soring agencies at an all day con-  
ference, Thursday, May 10 at Buffalo  
State College on Elmwood Ave.

Dr. Mary Frances Berry, member of the  
U.S. Commission on Civil Rights is  
the keynote speaker at the confer-  
ence, "Racial Justice: Facets of  
Racism in our Community." Dr. Berry  
openly opposed President Reagan's  
attempt to dismantle the commission.  
Dr. Berry's keynote presentation  
will open the conference, and will  
be followed by workshops on Racism  
in Education, moderated by Dr. John  
Dixon, Racism in the Media, with  
Mrs. Frances L. Murphy, Racism in  

the Political Process, with Jackie  

Rushton and the impact of Racism on  
the Economy, with Geraldine Memmo.  
Ramona Gallagher, Assistant Commis-  
sioner, Department of Labor, will  
be the lunch speaker. Workshops  
will be offered in an afternoon  
session also. Tickets for the  
conference are $20.00, and include  
Dr. Berry's presentation, workshops,  
lunch, a historical perspective on  
racism by Phyllis Truran, member of  
the YWCA Board of Directors, and a  
conference evaluation. For reserva-  
tions contact Pastora Middleton, at  
the YWCA, 852-6120, before April 30.  
Ms. Jan Peters, Director, Buffalo  
Federations and Neighborhood Centers,  
chairs the event.
