Women & the Law Conference  

This year's national conference will  
be held at the University of Wiscon-  
sin Law School during March 24-27.  
Featured speakers are: Elain Noble,  
Rep. Elizabeth Holtzman, Professor  
Herma Hill Kay, and feminist singer  
Margie Adam.  
The conference will have close to  
150 seminars dealing with such sub-  
jects as Lesbian Rights, Third World  
Women, Careers, Women in Health, the  
ERA, Labor Unions and Women in Pris-  
ons.  
For further information, write:  
Women & the Law Conference, Univ. of  
Wisconsin Law School, Madison, Wis-  
consin, 53706  

The Association for Women's  
Concerns of the University of Virgin-  
ia is sponsoring a symposium on "Wo-  
men and Work" on March 25 in the New-  
comb Hall Ballroom at U. Va. Topics  
for discussion will include the work  
situation for women and the direc-  
tions women must go to eliminate  
"pink collar ghettos".

The Women's Resource and Services  
Center, a cooperative project of the  

YMCA and Total Action Against Poverty,  
is planning a Women's Art Festival.  
The festival, planned for March 6-12,  
will be a celebration for women in  
the Arts and to commemorate our 2nd  
anniversary.  

The celebration theme is Artemis, a  
mythical lunar goddess. The week's  
festivities will include: visual  
arts showing, a publication of poet-  
ry, nightly readings of poetry, mus-  
ic, and drama presentations.  

Anyone interested in participating -  
contact Ms. Nolan, 342-4076, 343-6016,  
345-3947.
