QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS ON TRADES APPRENTICESHIP
WITH THE CITY AND COUNTY

What is Apprenticeship?

Apprenticeship is a program to pro-
vide you an opportunity to learn a
skilled trade and become a journeyman
through on-the-job training and class-
room instruction. Under an agreement
signed by you (sanctioned by your
Union) and the City, the City agrees
to teach you skills of the trade and
you, the apprentice, agree to work
and apply yourself to learn the trade.
How Long is the Program?

Depending on the trade, you will
spend from 3 to 4 years of on-the-job
training and attending required class-
room instruction on your own time.

If you have had prior training or ex-
perience or both, you may be granted
credit for such training or experience
by the Joint Apprenticeship Committee.
This may shorten your apprenticeship
training.

How Do I Become An Apprentice?

Our City's Civil Service Department
Recruitment Center can advise you of
the types of apprentice trades which
are available. You may request to
have your name placed on our recruit-
ment notification list for the trade
of your interest. This assures your
being notified of the next open filing
period. Other means by which we let
you know of open filing periods are by
posting examination announcements on
departmental, unions and Civil Service
bulletin boards. For those apprentice
opportunities which may be opened to
the general public in addition to City
employees, we also distribute the an-
nouncements to public libraries, Satel-
lite City Halls, and other strategic
places. We also place classified ad-
vertisements on Mondays in the daily
newspapers for those apprentice oppor-
tunities which may be opened to the
public.

Those selected will be given a medi-
cal examination by our physicians to
see that they are in proper health to
perform the duties of the trade.

During your apprenticeship training,
periodic evaluations will be made of
your progress on the job and in the
classroom. You will also have to make
periodic reports of your progress.

Upon successful completion of the
Trades Apprenticeship Program, you be-
come a fully qualified tradesperson.
What Salary Do I Receive?

All those selected for apprentice-
ship training will enter the program
at the Trades Apprentice I (WB5) level.
However, if you are now working for
the City government as a Helper in the
specified trade and your training and
experience as evaluated by the Joint
Apprenticeship Committee qualifies you
for a higher level,

Trades Apprentice level. For example,
if you are now an Electrician Helper
(WB 5) and qualify as a Trades Appren-
tice II - Electrician (WB6), you will
start at the Trades Apprentice II
level and your pay will be adjusted
accordingly.

If you had previous training and
experience in private industry, other
governmental agencies or the City in
the specified trade, you will enter
the program at the Trades Apprentice I
level. Your previous training and ex-
perience will be evaluated by the
Joint Apprenticeship Committee at the
request of the Department. You may be
advanced to any appropriate apprentice
level, at any time, if your previous
training or experience is creditable
in the trade.
