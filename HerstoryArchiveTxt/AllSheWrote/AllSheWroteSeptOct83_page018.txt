ALL SHE WROTE

letter of sympathy

he showed me his room.

one wall collaged with bright papers,
strips of headlines.

a cockbox stocked with small treasures.
this man was 70, not 27.

his salad buffet was a maze of delight--

twinkling laughter in the hearts of palm.

Margaret did the dishes while he and I
philosophized in the living room.

her small eyes glinted in a hammy face.
I remember those fat pink ankles
stuffed and footbound into chunky heels
like my grandma, like Petunia Pig.

once he called her in

and told me about

the architect she'd worked for

while she showed pictures of buildings
the man had designed.

my sister writes:

Paul died this month, seventy-seven.
and I write Margaret, not knowing
really, if we are kin.

so Margaret, in deepest sympathy
for all I could not say
for what you probably could not hear.

our strengths are in
different languages...
may yours

carry you through.

afternoon reaches in

to tint edges bronze

to pull me off into leaf shadows
silently dancing

to pull me back

to sun-sky noon
with all our senses

being

with all our senses

gelatinous and glowing
