SOME HERSTORY of the YWCA on OAHU  

1900 Ms. B.F. Dillingham and a group of five women met and organized the YWCA in Honolulu  

1917 Fernhurst Beach Club on Kakia Road in Waikiki  

1921 First Fernhurst YWCA Residence on King Street. Jubia Morgan was the architect.  

1925 Student work begun at University of Hawaii by Gink Reserve Department  

1927  

1930 The Kokokahi Camp site was given to the YWCA by the Atherton family  

1932 The Normal School became the Junior College of the University and the Blue  
Triangle Club became the University YWCA  

1936 Camp Halekipa, Kaneohe Bay Drive, opened for summer and weekend camping  

1946 The University YWCA received its charter from the National YWCA, became a fully  
accredited association, and moved to Atherton House  

1949 "Hale Wahine" built: first "permanent" home of the University YWCA  

1940 Richards Street building dedicated and opened. Jubia Morgan was the architect.
