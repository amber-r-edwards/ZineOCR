A colorful host of Asian goddesses  
art will descend upon Honolulu for a show-  
ing of contemporary silkscreen prints  
by Japanese-born artist Mayumi Oda.  
"Goddesses: Visions of Woman" compris-  
es twenty-three large prints executed  
from 1975 through 1982. Scheduled for  
November 14-24, 1982 at the University  
of Hawaii Commons Art Gallery on the  

Y Manoa campus, the show is the first  

opportunity for Hawaii's community to  
experience this artist's remarkable  
personal statement.  

Upon graduation from Tokyo Universi-  
ty of Fine Arts in 1966, Mayumi Oda  
moved to the United States and em-  
barked on a new life. Since then she  
has participated in many one-woman  
shows of her work in Japan and the  
United States, showing in several in-  
ternational print exhibitions as well.  
Mayumi's work is represented in these  
major collections: Museum of Modern  
Art, New York; Museum of Fine Arts,  
Boston; Library of Congress; and the  
Cincinnati Art Museum. A frequent vi-  
sitor to Japan, Mayumi currently lives  
on the outskirts of San Francisco  
near the Zen Center's Green Gulch Farm  
where she practices Zen meditation.  

Mayumi's dual cultural background--  
her native Japan and adopted America--  
provides the context for her integra-  
tion of the traditional and modern,
