TO MY FEMINIST SISTERS ON THE EVE OF THE ERA DEADLINE (June 30, 1982)

It has been forty years now that I have been a woman.  
Most of those years were enduring my womanness;  
These past few years have been celebrating my womanness.  

I celebrate that I never went to college to become a better wife,  
and when I finally went it was for me and done with excellence.  
I celebrate the active role I take in my life, my decisions,  

the meeting of my needs.  

I celebrate not only my divorce 10 years ago, but the time just before  
that act that I took a platter of meatloaf and dumped it upon  

his ugly head.  

I celebrate not only driving the truck I was once denied, but needing  
to drive it in my nursery business & having wondering men  

not understand.  

I celebrate the thinking of my woman children, who will carry the  
torch of feminism when my flame weakens in later years.  
I celebrate the enduring love of an aware man who respects my  

strengths, supports my strivings.  

I celebrate you, my sisters, my bulwark against sexism, my community  
that celebrates with me as I grow & learn & accomplish.  
You are the well-spring from which I am nourished.
