HAWAII WOMEN AGAINST INCEST

STATEMENT OF BELIEFS

We believe that incest is a crime
whose victims are overwhelmingly fe-
male. We believe that almost every
woman has been either a victim of in-
cest or her life has been affected by
incest. We believe that males are by
far the major perpetrators of incest.
We believe that in an incestuous situ-
ation if anyone has to leave the "home"
it should be the abuser--NOT the vic-
tim. We believe that incest is a re-
sult of the oppression of women and
works to maintain their oppression.
We believe that only women can and
will stop incest.

HAWAII ORGANIZATIONAL FOUNDATION
Purpose: To stop incest by supporting
and advocating for female incest survi-
vors.

Goals:

1) To provide women with accurate in-
formation about incest.

2) To share skills for actively deal-
ing with its occurrence.

3) To share methods that allow women
to deal effectively with sexual oppres-
sion.

4) To use the consensus process for
decision making.

5) To share knowledge about how to
handle victimizing situations in adult
life.

6) To collect and disseminate informa-
tion on incest and how it occurs.

7) To explore the feminist politics of
incest.

8) To act as a credible information
source for the community.

Activities:

1) Support group meetings once a week.
The activities below are open to all
concerned women (decision making and
leadership as stated below).

2) Advocacy and action group.

3) Newsletter.

4) Research group.

5) Theory group to explore the sexual
politics of incest and formulate strat-
egies for combating incest.

6) Outreach through word of mouth,
newspaper, women's organizations.

7) Fundraising.

8) Public speaking in the community.

9) Herstory group to record the history
of HWAI.

10) Lobbying.

HWAI SUPPORT GROUP FUNCTION AND ORGANI-
ZATION

The support group is open to any
woman survivor of incest. (All other
activities of HWAI are open to con-
cerned women.) A woman's membership
in the group will be anonymous; all
things said in the group are confiden-
tial unless otherwise agreed upon.

The group functions as a self-help
group. This means that members take
an active part in supporting and help-
ing each other. The group should re-
main small to provide a safe and com-
fortable atmosphere for discussions.
Decisions will be made by consensus.

By this we mean that all women present
must agree to decisions before they be-
come final. There are no leaders of
the group. If such a function becomes
necessary (such as coordinator or
spokeswoman) the position will rotate
among members so all have a chance to
share in the responsibility and experi-
ence.

Who: Hawaii Women Against Incest (HWAI)

As an indigenous Hawaiian woman who
was fortunate enough to attend this
conference as a staff person, I re-
turned home with a much broader poli-

the dynamics that are common to all

erful nations reads essentially the

Ultimately, the conference left me
feeling strong and optimistic. The
growing pan Pacific consciousness of
the movement, the increasing visibili-
ty of women, and the huge strides we
have taken despite geographic separa-
tion, cultural differences, very lit-
tle money, a huge range of issues, and
the sheer power of the entity we are
fighting against. As our roles are be-
coming more clear and our vision more
attainable, a nuclear free, oppression
