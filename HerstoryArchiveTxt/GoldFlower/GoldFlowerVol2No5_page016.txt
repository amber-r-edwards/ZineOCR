AN EDUCATION

DIRECTORY

Toni McNaron, an associate
professor of English at the
University of Minnesota, has
been named to head the UM's
new Women's Studies Program,
an interdisciplinary program

within the College of Liberal

Arts.

McNaron, who has taught at
the U since 1964, holds a
bachelor's degree in English
and math from the University

of Alabama, a master's in Eng-
lish and intellectual history

from Vanderbilt University
and a doctorate in English
and English history from the
University of Wisconsin.

McNaron will continue to
hold her position with the
English department, as the
coordinator's position is
currently funded at only
half-time.

The Women's Studies Pro -
gram will offer this fall
WS 1-001, “Comparative Study
of Women,” an introductory

survey course to be taught by

sociology professor Caroline
Rose, political science pro-
fessor Joanne Arnaud and an-
thropology instructor Janet
Spector. The course will be
offered fall through spring
quarters.

U Extension

Several new women's studies
courses--and some reorganized
standard courses--are offer-
ed this fall by the U of M's
extension division for women.

The "Woman in America" sem-
inar this year will include
sessions on Gertrude Stein,
women in the arts, and his-
tory of American dance with
discussions directed by such
persons as sculptor Katherine
Nash, dancer Nancy Hauser and
women's studies coordinator
Toni McNaron. The course
meets Thursday mornings at
9:30 and Thursday evenings at
6:30, both in 302 Westbrook.

"A Woman's World," an intro-
ductory survey of lit-
erature by and about women,
will be offered on Wednesday
at 9:30 am in 302 Westbrook.
Julie Carson is faculty co-
ordinator; session topics
include George Eliot, Jane
Austen, contemporary women
poets and women in Norman
Mailer’s fiction.

"Woman: A Sense of Identi-
ty," a practicum in tech-
niques of self-affirmation,
will be offered at 6 pm
Tuesdays in 392 Westbrook.

"Guidelines for Women," a
self-direction course involv-
ing aptitude and interest
testing, will meet in five
sessions starting Oct. 9 at
McPhail Center.

Further information on
these programs can be obtain-
ed through the Women's Pro-
grams office, 290 Westbrook
Hall.

Metro State JC

Two courses in women's
studies will be repeated at
Metropolitan State Junior
College this fall, due to
increasing student demand.

Originally offered last
spring, both courses are
open to full and part-time
students; participants earn
four undergraduate credits
for each. Courses, which
cost $35, meet for a three-
hour lecture and one-hour
discussion each week. Class-
es run Sept. 24-Dec. 18.

"Women in the 1970's" will
meet on Tuesdays at 6:30 pm
to discuss socialization,
stereotyping and alterna-
tives to traditional roles.
Texts will include "Up
Against the Wall, Mother" by
Mary Louise Bisco and Elsie
Adams, and "Born Female" by
Caroline Bird.

"Advanced Critical Reading:
A View of Women as Reflected
in Current Fiction and Non-
Fiction" will meet at 1 pm
on Mondays, Wednesdays and
Fridays, with an additional
hour of discussion by ar-
rangement. Texts include
Lessing's "Golden Notebook,"
Chesler's "Women and Mad-
ness," Woolf's "A Room of
One's Own" and Eleanor Roose-
velt's "Autobiography."
Subjects for discussion in-
clude anthropological, psych-
ological and literary views
of women.

For more information on
these courses, call Mary
Scangarello, 335-8944, ext.
