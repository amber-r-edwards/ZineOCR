Women’s Film

WOMEN'S FILM CO-OP; 200 Main St., Northampton, MA 01060, (413) 586-2011, distributes feminist films throughout the country and publishes a catalogue listing their own films as well as others with information on where to obtain them. Please send a donation for the catalogue.

A coalition of community and college women in St. Paul invites the people of the Twin Cities to WOMEN IN FOCUS. This two-part film festival presents a variety of films by and about women. These films will be shown the 23rd and 25th of January at the Macalester College Student Union, on Grand and Snelling Avenues, in St. Paul at 7:30 p.m. Admission is 75 cents.

Film Coop--Northampton, Mass.: Special--This week only--"Women's Film". Produced by The Massachusetts Coop is available for rent--call Dan at 217-225-243-624. Fee negotiable. Regularly $60 per film. Our group is willing to loan it out cheaply to interested groups to help make up extra costs from our own feminist film festival. It is 40 mm, contains women from many different classes, races, and age groups speaking about their lives and what they are doing to change them. You can do something constructive for your group and help another struggling feminist venture as well, by calling Jerry at 644-4404 or Cers at 698-6837 for rental information.

Wednesday, January 23, features Joanne Woodward in "Rachel, Rachel". In addition, "Day of Plane Hunting", a documentary showing the role of Vietnamese women in the Indochina war.

Friday, January 25, offers the feature, "Black Women," a NET production in which Nikki Giovanni, Roberta Flack, and Lena Horne talk about the black woman's relationship to black men, white society, and the feminist movement. Both nights will also show a selection of filmmakers, including the Twin Cities Women's Collective.
