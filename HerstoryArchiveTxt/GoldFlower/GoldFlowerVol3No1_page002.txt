A residential street, a

a pleasantly decorated blue  
and white living room, femin-  
ist posters on the walls; I  
am immediately suspicious.  
I'm not supposed to feel at  
ease in an employment agency.  

The goals of Feminist For-  
mations are as disconcerting-  
ly healthy as their environ-  
ment. Their standards demand  
that they place skilled women  
in non-traditional, enjoyable  
temporary jobs that pay well.  
Too often, the three founders  
/counselors say, employment  
agencies push a person into  
any job at any pay scale.  
Rarely do they find a suita-  
ble position for an individu-  
al. Lynn McGuigan says they  
hope to place a woman in a  
job where "her potential will  
come out, not be stashed  
away."  

In order to do that, they  
must first ascertain what a  
woman's potentials are. They  
find that women often have a-  
bilities they don't know they  
have or experience that  
they undervalue. This is es-  
pecially true of women return-  
ing to the workforce after  
raising a family. A woman may  
not realize the validity of,  
for example, her community or-  
ganizing experience. She may  
be diffident about "selling  
herself." Feminist Forma-  
tions, as a go-between with  
the employer, can "sell" the  
employee, presenting her in a  
positive manner. "We don't  
have to tell the employer she  
did it without pay," says  
Carol Lee. They can play up  
her qualifications.

THE FUTURE  

They feel that their work  
will also help women in the  
long run. "Like a bank that  
offers a new service," as the  
advantages become apparent,  
the concept will spread. As  
women begin to understand  
that they don't really have  
to accept low-paying, boring  
jobs, they will stop seeking  
that kind. As employers no-  
tice that women have varied  
skills and work well in non-  
traditional temporary jobs,  
they will hire more women.  

As employment agencies see  
the success of this approach  
to employment, more of them  
will adopt it.  

They also hope to raise  
the general pay scale for wo-  
men city-wide by decreasing  
the supply of women who must  
accept low-paying jobs.  

This employment agency is  
revolutionary even in non-fem-  
inist ways. The idea of em-  
ploying "professionals" on a  
free-lance or temporary basis  
is not widely practiced. The  
counselors feel that if more  
people worked temporarily,  
they wouldn't get so bored  
with their jobs since they'd  
be gone before the "honeymoon"  
was over.  

In the month and a half  
that the agency has been in  
operation, its largest areas  
of concentration and therefore  
success have been construction  
and the creative fields--wri-  
ting, advertising, interior  
decorating.  

Women like construction jobs  
because they pay more than "women's" jobs,  
and because, as Lee says,  
"Women are intrigued by the  
idea of spending the whole day  
outside in the sun." They  
like the physically strength-  
ening activity. Lee says they  
try to send women to construc-  
tion jobs in teams of at least  
two for mutual support in an  
emphatically male situation.
