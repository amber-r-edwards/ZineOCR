"The state Human Rights Board has made a
finding of discrimination in the decision of
an Austin radio and television station not to
hire a woman for a news job.

"The complaint was filed March 6, 1973, by
Elizabeth R. Watson against station KAUS.
Mrs. Watson contended she was promised a
broadcast news job but then was not hired by
news director Gary Peterson because of her
sex.

"State Human Rights Commissioner Samuel
Richardson had found that there was not prob-
able cause for a finding of discrimination.
His decision was overruled by the board af-
ter an appeal by Mrs. Watson.

"Thomas Donaldson, deputy human rights com-
missioner, said the case will be reinvesti-
gated prior to setting up a conciliation
meeting.

"Donaldson said the board's finding was is-
sued Aug. 20. He said the board found that
discrimination existed but declined to re-
lease the board's decision in full."

"Some 150 University of Minnesota Hospital
employees will get monthly pay increases of
$85 to $100 based on an agreement to end "sex
discrimination" against a predominantly fe-
male group of custodial workers, a union off-
icial said.

"(Paul Goldberg, director of American Feder-
ation of State, County and Municipal Employ-
ees) said that previously a predominantly fe-
male group of hospital employees, classed as
custodial workers, were paid $440 to $550 a
month. The hospital janitor classification,
mostly male employees, received $525 to $650
a month, he said.

"The custodial workers will be reclassified
to the new position of hospital custodian at
the same pay as janitors, he said.

"Our concern was, right on the surface, sex
discrimination because cleaning is cleaning,"
he said. "In the past we heard arguments
that a janitor does all the heavy work--run-
ning the buffer and big mops--while custodial
workers do only light chores. But it was our
judgment that there's not $100 a month diff-
erence between the two positions."
