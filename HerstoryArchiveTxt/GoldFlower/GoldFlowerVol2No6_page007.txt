There's going to be a "cooperative" con-
ference on Sept. 28-30 in Mpls. People from:
all over will be there and who knows what
will happen. Most of the workshops will be
planned when everyone gets to the People's
Center, 20th and Riverside, on Fri. night.

I work at a food coop and the only reason
I'm planning to go is because some women
will be organizing, probably around noon Sat
(place to be arranged). We are hoping that
we can meet each other and share our experi-
ences as women working in coops.

There are lots of women doing lots of hard
work in the coops. There are lots who used
to work in coops. And there are women who
have intentionally stayed away from coop
involvement. From what I know, even though
many women want to work cooperatively, the
coops are still traditionally sexist and may
keep many women away. I find this showing
up especially in the way people at our coop
talk to each other at meetings.

Although there is a lot more that I could
say, as a feminist, about my own coop exper-
iences, I would rather not write about it by
myself. I would like to talk more to other
women and then write collectively. It would
be exciting if through the conference we
could get together a group of women from
every coop to analyze sexism in coops and
what we can do about it. Maybe we could
then write a thing for "Gold Flower."

For information about places and times call
any of the coops listed in this issue. The
conference is open to anyone and gay care
is provided.

Linda Thain and Jeanne Henjum
