Emma, West Germany's glossy lib-

"Q. Let's start with who you
are?

"A. ZORA 1: If this is a person-
al question, then we are women
between the ages of 20 and 51.
Some of us sell our labour, some
of us take what we need, and
others are 'parasites' on the
welfare state. Some have chil-
dren, some don't. Some women
are lesbians, others love men.
We buy in disgusting supermar-
kets, we live in ugly houses, we
like going for walks or to the
cinema, the theater, or the
disco. We have parties and cul-
tivate idleness. And of course
we live with the contradiction
that many things we want to do
can't be done spontaneously.

But after successful actions we
have great fun!

"Q. What does your name mean?
"A. ZORA 2: 'The Red Zora and
Her Gang' [a children's book]--
that is the wild street kid who
steals from the rich to give to
the poor. Until today it seems
a male privilege to build gangs
or to act outside the law. Yet
particularly because girls and
women are strangled by thousands
of personal and political chains
this should make us masses of
'bandits' fighting for our free-
dom, our dignity, and our human-
ity. Law and order are funda-
mentally against us, even if we
have hardly achieved any rights
and have to fight for them
daily. Radical women's strug-
gles and loyalty to the law--
there's no way they go together!

"Q. How important is the con-

"A. ZORA 1: In all our attacks

We don't struggle for women in

modern slave trade has its equi-

of oppression are different but
they all have the same roots.
Nobody can play cards with us as
chips any longer.

"The separation between men
and women has its equivalent in-
ternationally in the separation
between people of the First and
Third World. We ourselves
profit from the international
division of labor. We want to
break our involvement with this
system, and understand our
common interests with women from
other countries."

movement struggle for the aboli-

vision of the old law was
rejected by the West German
Supreme Court. The very next
day Red Zora women struck for
the first time, bombing the
Supreme Court building in
Karlsruhe.
