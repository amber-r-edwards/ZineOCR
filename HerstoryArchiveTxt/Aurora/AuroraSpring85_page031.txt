Bios:

Rhea Hirshman lives in New Haven where she has been an English teacher and

a freelance writer, and where she now owns Golden Thread Booksellers. She is
unnaturally attached to her cats, her Subaru, and her Supremes’ records.

Annemarie Eileraas wears a black leather jacket and eats Captain Crunch for
breakfast every morning.

Paul Massi Cameli received his BA in Women’s Studies and Sociology from Yale in
1984. He works in New Haven in a group home for mentally handicapped people.
Tod Hill just completed a senior essay on social and political relationships be-
tween lesbians and gay men in the 1950s. He lives with his lover, Paul Massi
Cameli, and no cats. Paul and Tod are not lesbians.  
Missy Park said that we should write something for her. She is a very cute girl with
a nice tattoo.

Liz Schindler spends most of her time painting and typing on the Macintosh. She is

a senior, and can’t wait to go out West.

Dominique Dibbell is at this very moment waitressing in Berkeley at the Brick Hut
Café. Aren’t you jealous.

Sylvia Brownrigg likes to write about herself in the third person.

Yvonne Zylan, a non-native speaker of Japanese, is often found on the softball
field. She lives off campus for a reason.
