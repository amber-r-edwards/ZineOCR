Bisia (1896-1981)

for my Grandmother

the plastic transparent oxygen mask

swallowed half your face
pressed into your bloated neck

ruptured your limp mouth with rhythmic gasps

morphine infant

I tried to stare into your open eyes

now each night I rip at your
oxygen mask

I tear it away and
press my mouth to your lips
I inhale your sour steam

I breathe
and wait through the dark
for your eyes to meet mine.
