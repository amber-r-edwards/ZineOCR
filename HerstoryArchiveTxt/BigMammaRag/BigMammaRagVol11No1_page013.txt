will improve for their people.

*The Sacred Shawl Society,  
a group of traditional Indian  
women at Pine Ridge, S.D.,  
work in their community to  
counsel and support Indian  
women in crisis situations.  
Roberta Arkeketa, Director of  
their 24-hour Crisis center  
in Pine Ridge states, "In  
Indian society the women were  
considered equal just as the  
children were. In order for  

the tree of life to bloom you  
need all three and none is  
excluded from the sacred  
circle. We see things that  
have to be done within our  
nation to strengthen and nour-  
ish it so it will continue to  
grow instead of diminishing  
it, and each and every one of  
us play an important role in  
the future of our nation whe-  
ther you be man, woman or  
child."

*The Women's Dance birthing  
crew in Minneapolis, Minn.  
serves an urban Indian comm-  
unity with birthing alterna-  
tives and women's health care  
based on traditional Indian  
values. The women of the crew,  
of necessity, have focused  
much of their work on alcohol-  
ism, domestic violence and  
sexual abuse. The women have  
lunar calendars and station-  
ery available for sale which  
they produced themselves to  
support their work. They do  
consulting, counselling,  
workshops and a class series  
and they can be contacted at:  
Women's Dance Birth Crew, c/o  
2517 16th Ave. South, Minnea-  
polis, Minn. 55404.

*Native Nevadans for Politi-  
cal Education and Action was  
founded primarily by Indian  
women in 1978 to provide a  
vehicle for political educa-  
tion and organizing of Indian  
communities in Nevada. They  
have been working to oppose  
the MX missile system in Nev-  
ada, and in educating their  
People on nuclear, chemical  
and other environmental is-  
sues. NNPEA represents an im-  

portant function of Native  
Women's movement.

plans to develop alternative  
housing, food supply and eco-  
nomies based on self-suffi-

The Color Purple is a cele-  
bration of life, and I say  
this not only because it ends  
happily. Walker is an affirm-  
ative writer in the best  
sense, because of the love  
with which she creates these  
people, imbuing them with dis-  
tinctive personalities, vivid  
bodies and memorable voices.

Despite the book's weakness  
- Nettie's letters - the auth-  
or's generosity of spirit tou-  
ches everything with a kind of  
grace. In her earlier novels  
there was much compassion and  
growth, but in The Color Pur-  
ple there is more sheer, abun-  
dant joy. It's a book that ma-  
kes you want to cry - and sing.
