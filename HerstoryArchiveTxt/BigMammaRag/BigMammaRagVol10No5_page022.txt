Mitsuye And Nellie - Asian  
American Poets creates a mo-  
ving and challenging double  
portrait of two women whose  
poetry expresses, with drama-  
tic clarity, the immigrant  
experience of Asian American  
women in a society contemp-  
tuous and suspicious of  
"Orientals." It is a film  
about tenderness and anger  
between mothers and daughters,  
generational conflicts and  
the breaking of stereotyped  
images of Asian American wo-  
men. Available from Light-  
Saraf Films, 131 Concord St.,  
San Francisco, CA 94112. 58  
minutes, 16mm color. Rental  
is $75, inquire regarding  
benefit showings.

We are a Women's Press in St.  
Louis, MO. We want to print  

a booklet on writings and  
drawings from women locked up  
in prisons and jails. We are  
looking for a group of women  
who are locked up now and ex-  
cons to edit the booklet. If  
you have any articles, let-  
ters, journal writings, short  
stories, poetry, comics,  
drawings, or other graphics  
you want to publish, please  
send them to us. If you are  
interested in helping edit  
the material, let us know.  
Please spread the word to  
women you know who are locked  
up. And write us if you have  
any questions or contribu-  
tions. Tiamat Press, 6344  
Rosebury, Clayton, MO 63130.

La Mujer en la Sociedad  
Puertorriquena by Edna Acos-  
ta-Belen examines the status  
of women in Puerto Rican so-  
ciety. Chapters address  
feminism, labor market, fic-  
tion, sexual relations. Eng-  
lish and Spanish bibliogra-  
phy includes references from  
throughout Latin America.  
Write for the price, from  
Ediciones Huracan, Inc,  
Avenida Gonzales 1002, Rio  
Piedras, Puerto Rico.

Nice Jewish Girls is a new  
book from Persephone Press  
(publishers of This Bridge  
Called My Back). The contri-  
butors-Jewish lesbians and  
non-lesbians, Afro-Judaic,  
Arabic, Ashkenazi, Sephardic-  
proudly affirm Jewish identi-  
ty and confront anti-Semitism  
inside and outside the women's  
movement. $8.95 from P.O. Box  
7222, Watertown, MA 02172.

Art Works For People, a coa-  
lition of artists for social  
change, is organizing a con-  
ference for progressive ar-  
tists of all disciplines to  
be held May 15-16. The con-  
ference will seek ways to  
tie the arts into struggles  
and movements for social  
change. For more info: call  
(415) 527-1401. It will be  
held at the Women's Building  
in San Francisco.

International Conference on  
Psychiatric Oppression will  
be held at the U. of Toronto  
on May 14-18. The theme is  
Internationalism and a poster  
is available in different lan-  
guages. It is open to all for-  
mer "mental patients" and  
also to anti-psychiatry acti-  
vists who have been endorsed  
by an ex-patient organization.  
No mental health workers may  
attend unless they have pre-  
viously been incarcerated.  
Coincidentally, the American  
Psychiatric Assoc. will be  
having their conference in  
Toronto then too. Registra-  
tion is $80 and includes room  
and meals. For info contact:  
On Our Own, Box 7251, Station  
A, Toronto, Ontario, Canada  
MSW 1X9. Phone #(416) 362-0200.
