Nestle Boycott

While the passage of the  
World Health Organization  
(WHO) code is a victory,  
there is no way to enforce  
its standards. Nestle has re-  
fused to change its practices  
voluntarily to abide by the  
WHO Code. (Nestle claims to  
agree with the code's broad  
aim but refuses to follow  
the specific requirements of  
its provisions.)

For the past 34 months,  
Nestle has refused to meet  
with INFACT and its support-  
ers. It is clear that Nestle  
and the Reagan administration  
put profit before people, and  
will be most effectively at-  
tacked by a continued boycott  
campaign which will hit them  
where it hurts the most - in  
sales.

Faith Ringgold (b. 1934)  
is an artist interested in  
capturing "the conceptual vi-  
sions of the Black image in-  
herent in the sculpture and  
masks of African art".

Ringgold has long been in-  
volved in the struggle for  
equality for women, and she  
feels that her most profound  
artistic contribution has  
been in women's art: "My art  
is for everyone but it is  
about me (my sisters)." In  
Aunts Edith and Bessie, from  
her Family of Women series,  
Ringgold shapes soft, pliable  
materials into life-sized hu-  
man figures; the open mouths  
of these two subjects are  
meant to symbolize the need  
for women to speak out for  

themselves.  
There will be a show of Faith  
Ringgold's work at the U. of  
Colorado at Boulder in early  
October. She will be in town  
and will do a Mask perform-  
ance. Call the U. for more  
information.
