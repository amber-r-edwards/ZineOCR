films

La Operación, a 30-minute,  
color documentary film focusing  
on the widespread sterilization  
of Puerto Rican women is under  
production. Filmmaker Ana Garcia  
explores U.S. financial involvement  
in sterilization programs through  
population control policies  
encouraged by the Puerto Rican  
and U.S. governments. Funds are  
needed to complete the film. Send  
tax-deductible contributions to The  
Film Fund - Sterilization Abuse  
Project, 80 E. 11th St., New  
York, NY 10003.

La Frontera is a 25-minute  
slide show on Spanish-speaking  
undocumented workers in  
the United States. The 140  
slides and the narrative examine  
reasons for emigration,  
the workers' fight for justice  
and the role of U.S. government  
and industry along the  
Mexican-U.S. border. Rental:  
$30 per week. Available in  
Spanish or English, New Mexico  
People and Energy, P.O. Box  
4726, Albuquerque, NM 87196.

Speak Up, Say No! is a 6-minute  
filmstrip with cassette and  
teaching guide, which helps  
teach children how to avoid  
sexual abuse. The filmstrip  
teaches children not only to  
beware of strangers, but to  
recognize and avoid sexual  
abuse from trusting friends  
and relatives. From Krause  
House, P.O. Box 880,  
Oregon City, OR 97045.

Women's Self-Defense: Options  
and Strategies, an introductory-  
level pamphlet on self-defense,  
is available in English and  
Spanish from the Women's Alliance  
Press, P.O. Box 1011, Davis,  
CA 95627. The pamphlet is  
directed at women who have  
never taken self-defense and  
explains basic self-defense  
techniques. For a sample copy  
and order form send $1 and  
specify if you want the English  
or Spanish edition.

Two new pamphlets on women in  
Latin America now available:  
“Women in Peru" describes the  
reality of paper, pet seller,  
domestic worker, and the  
recent return to a free  
democracy that has hardly touched  
women's position. In "Military  
Ideology and the Dissolution  
of Democracy (Women in Chile)"  
the author points at the  
powerlessness of women in Chile  
where even the banned political  
parties have no programs  
for women's advancement. $1.50  
each from Change, Parnell House,  
25 Wilton Rd, London SW1V 1JS  
England.
