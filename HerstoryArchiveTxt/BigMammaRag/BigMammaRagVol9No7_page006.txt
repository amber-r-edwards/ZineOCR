wearing just a scanty wrap
standing at the top of an
aircraft aisle with men seat-

know which airline you were
flying with?" The president

The People Speak

In May this year, feminists
in Queensland, Australia,
successfully lobbied an anti-
abortion bill out of the
Queensland parliament. The
proposed legislation denied
women the right to choose
abortion and defended the
rights of the unborn child.

During the protracted fight
to have the legislation
thrown out, a new set of sur-
vey results were printed in
the Australian Women's Week-
ly which showed that the
majority of Australian wo-
men favored a policy of
free abortion on demand.
On a national level, 88%
thought that abortion should
be freely available, 11%
felt that abortion should
be freely available only
if there was a health risk,
and only 1% registered a NO
to this question.

Disposable Workers

Disabled and mentally im-
paired people are being hired
at minimum wage to clean up
the poisonous chemicals in the
Love Canal area near Buffalo,
N.Y. Love Canal is where
Hooker Chemical dumped some
of the most toxic chemicals
known. The plant plans to
start the clean-up without
waiting for the Environmental
Protection Agency's report on
whether the area can be cleared
without hurting or killing
the workers.

More Victims

ports that while the count-
ry's attention has been fo-
cused on the murders of child-
ren in Atlanta, that city has
also witnessed an alarming
number of unsolved murder
cases involving teenage girls
and women.

According to the Times, at
least 38 unsolved killings of
Atlanta-area females have oc-
curred since late 1978. Most
of the victims--who ranged in
age from 14 to 60-- died from
stabbing or strangulation.

"It is believed by some
observers that the number of
unsolved slayings of females
would exceed 38 if victims
whose bodies were found in
all suburbs and neighboring
counties also were counted.
That procedure is already
used in the widely publicized
case of black youths."

Fascist Fade-in

The Chinese custom of drown-
ing baby girls at birth is ap-
parently still going on.
Authorities outside China
had believed that the practice
had faded away after the Comm-
unists gained power in 1949.
However, an official family
planning document by the Chi-
nese government says other-
wise.

The document says that the
continuation of the custom
shows that many Chinese--ap-
parently mainly in rural areas
--still value male children
more than female offspring,
because of the lingering idea
that boys will be more useful
workers.

The paper published in the
latest issue of Population
Studies journal, gave no esti-
mate of how many female child-
ren are killed each year by
their parents.
