1975

FDA approves marketing of a modified Dalkon Shield. At least
13 deaths and 223 infected miscarriages have been attributed
to the Dalkon Shield. The FDA does not require efficacy or
safety tests for most IUDs, including the Shield because they
are classified as devices, not drugs, even though they are
implanted in the body.

The Supreme Court decided that states may
impose restrictions requiring women under 18
to obtain parental consent for an abortion. And in a 6-3 rul-
ing in the same case, the court held that a woman's husband
need not consent to her abortion.

BeBe K'Roche finished recording first album with Olivia Records.

3,000 stage “loiter-in” in Dallas to support street-walkers
being harassed by a city-wide crackdown on prostitution.

Women in Print Conference held in Omaha, Nebraska for women
working in all women's groups in the areas of feminist
publications, publishing, and bookstores.

The murder/armed robbery trial of Susan Saxe ended initially
in Boston with a hung jury.

The Federal law barring Medicaid payments for abortion was
overturned in Brooklyn by a judge ruling in the case of Cora
McRae who had filed a class-action suit so the ruling would
apply to all women involved in the Medicaid program.

Lureida Torres freed after over 4 months in Federal custody
for her refusal to cooperate with a grand jury conducting an
investigation into the Puerto Rican Independence movement in
the United States.

Tallahassee Feminist Women's Health Clinic went into court
against five OB/Gyn physicians in a case centering on the
attempts of the medical profession to monopolize women's
health care and abortion services.

A Federal Judge dismissed the suit filed by the Tallahassee
Feminist Health Center.

Supreme Court ruled that employers may refuse to pay dis-
ability benefits to women for pregnancy.

In the first comprehensive challenge to the treatment of
battered wives, 12 women filed suit in Manhattan Supreme
Court charging that they were denied help by police who refus-
ed to arrest their husbands.

1977

Our Bodies, Ourselves is published in Spanish with updated
materials as well as new content directly related to the
experiences of Chicana and Puerto Rican women.

Inez Garcia, acquitted after a three-month retrial with a
verdict of justifiable homicide in self-defense.

30 feminist health workers conduct an inspection of a hospital
in Tallahassee, FL that was aimed at establishing the right of
feminists and citizen's groups to inspect health facilities
and challenge the use of costly and dangerous technology in
normal births. Four women were later arrested and tried on
charges of criminal trespass.

Agents of Immigration and Naturalization Service raided Gerry
Garment factory in north Denver and arrested 4 Mejicanas.

Assata Shakur, a Black Liberation leader, is convicted of
several counts of murder and assault.

Feminists in Madison, WI successfully organized a recall elect-
ion of Judge Archie Simonson who had pardoned the actions of
high school boys who had gang raped a high school woman.

Bedford Hills Correctional Facility women demand in the U.S.
Court of Appeals that the preliminary injunction issued in June
1977 ordering male guards out of their living quarters be
upheld.

Joann Little disappeared from the North Carolina Correctional
Center for Women in Raleigh, NC where she was serving a sen-
tence for murder of a white jailer who tried to rape her in 1975.

Diana Press, the women's press in Oakland, CA was vandalized.

After a two-week work stoppage and sit-in, 211 housekeepers,
mostly Black and Puerto Rican women, employed by the Morrisania
Community Corp. in New York City, won the right to a union
certification election.
