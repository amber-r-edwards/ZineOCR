GROW VITAMINS

THE WILLMAR 8 is a film that tells
the inspiring story of eight women--
bank workers in the small town of
Willmar, Minnesota-- who suddenly
found themselves in the forefront
of the fight for working women's
rights. Like millions of other women
throughout the country, they had been
forced into low-wage, dead-end jobs.
When a young male trainee was hired
at twice their starting salary and
the women were required to "train
him in," they complained to the
bank president who told them, "We
are not all equal, you know." This
comment led the previously apolit-
ical women to form a union and start
the first bank strike in the history
of Minnesota. After two years, the
strike was eventually broken. The
film shows that through their strug-
gle, the Willmar 8 won a sense of
dignity and self-worth that had been
denied them on the job. The Willmar
8, directed by Lee Grant, is avail-
able from California Newsreel, 630
Natoma St., San Francisco, CA 94103.
(415) 621-6196. The film is 55 min-
utes and rents for $75. Those not
able to pay the fee may request a
discount.

The FEMINIST WRITER'S GUILD has
launched a nationwide petition drive
in protest of the new, more restric-
tive eligibility requirements for
the National Endowment for the Arts
Creative Writing Fellowships. In-
stead of broadening the NEA's cultural
base to reflect the literary life
of America in the 1980's, these new
standards institutionalize discrim-
ination against all women writers
and are particularly unfair to fem-
inist, lesbian, third world, and
working-class writers whose work re-
flects their experience. The Femin-
ist Writers Guild is circulating
petitions throughout the literary
community. For copies of the peti-
tion, please write to: Feminist
Writers Guild, Box 184, Village Sta-
tion, NYC, NY 10014. Enclose a self-
addressed stamped envelope.
