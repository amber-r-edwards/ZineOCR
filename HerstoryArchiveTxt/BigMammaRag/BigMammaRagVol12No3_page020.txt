Most prisoners confined against their will must rely on lawyers as their sole link to the courts and any hope of "justice." Prisoners are prey to unscrupulous court-appointed lawyers who show more concern for the payment they get than in getting justice to the prisoner. United Prisoner Personnel International (UPPI) has prepared a scathing essay detailing such practices and is of interest to anyone trying to help a prisoner through the legal system. Write for "American Justice: ABC's of How It Really Works" from UPPI, c/o William Sawyer, Ariz. State Prison, Box B40424, Florence, AZ 85232. Include an SASE.

A new poster distribution project is being sponsored by the Northland Poster Collective (NPC). Socially committed artists have a hard time getting our work out to a wide public. We believe that by pooling our efforts, we who create radical art can have a greater impact on our people's consciousness and increase the income that sustains our work. We've decided to expand our mail order distribution brochure, begun 4 years ago, into a larger catalog which can encompass the work of other artists from across the US and hopefully beyond. For more info write the NPC 1519 East Franklin Ave., Minn., MN 55404.

Writer/editor seeks women who have given children up for adoption--either legal or covert (for example, a child raised by another member of the family or a friend of the family as their own, without formal adoption procedures taking place) - willing to write or talk about their experiences for an anthology bringing together women's voices and historical, cross-cultural, legal and feminist perspectives on the institutions and customs surrounding adoption. I hope to focus on the complex forces, overt and tacit, social and economic, brought to bear on women's choices or lack of choices in adoption situations. Inquiries from any other women interested in working on the anthology are also welcome. Contact: K. Kaufman, c/o Plexus 545 Athol Ave., Oakland, CA 94606.

The APA epitomizes the dehumanizing attitudes and practices of institutional psychiatry: incarceration and forced "treatment," sanism, and the medical model explanation of problems of living.

The call is going out, initiated by the African People's Socialist Party and the Committee in Solidarity With African Independence, for all people wanting to work hard and take an active part in building a social movement to come to the front line in Oakland this summer. In Oakland, where there are 10,000 homeless black people the demand for Black Community Control has gone out and actual programs and struggle for housing is being carried on, teaching and involving the community and exposing property relations. Oakland is the national headquarters of the APSP and Black Community Control Committee which has the strategy, organization and tools to make the struggle and call for national and international participation and to make the battle of Oakland spark a nationwide movement. CSAI calls on North Americans to come to the Oakland Summer Project and participate firsthand in the solidarity movement. If you want to support the Project but can't come yourself, you can contribute to its success by sending donations, contacting organizations and institutions in your community that may want to send a representative or sponsor someone to come. Call or Write: CSAI c/o Uhuru House, 7622 MacArthur Blvd., Oakland, CA 94605-- (415) 562-1920 or (415) 282-7670.
