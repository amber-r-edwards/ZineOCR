1972  

Diana Press organized  

Of Our Backs reports that three out of nine Washington, D.C. hospitals surveyed still refuse to treat or examine victims of rape.  

Angela Davis goes on trial for murder and conspiracy. Charges resulted from a courtroom escape attempt in which a judge, an escapee, and an accomplice were killed.  

Equal Employment Opportunity Act signed into law by Richard Nixon (notice the big difference?)  

Preview issue of MS. hits the news stands.  
National Lesbian Information Service is formed.  
Women's Strike Day celebrated nationwide.  

Big Mama Rag begins publishing  

Yvonne Wanrow arrested for killing a known rapist who had threatened her and her children.  

Police raid the Los Angeles Feminist Health Center, seizing files, birth control pills, and a carton of strawberry yogurt.  

Feminist Book Club dissolves due to “irreconcilable differences.”  

Carol Downer of the Los Angeles Feminist Health Center arraigned on the charge of "practicing medicine without a license."  

First national convention of Gynecological Self Help Clinics in Iowa City, IA.  

Richard Nixon re-elected by a landslide.  

Mountain States Women's Abortion Coalition holds "Crimes Against Women Tribunal" in Denver.  

Women's Voice Bookstore opens in Denver.  
Carol Downer acquitted.  
1500 women attend Fourth National Women's Conference held in 1973.  

First New Women's Survival Catalog (the big red one) published.  

U.S. Supreme Court rules 7-2 that state laws restricting early abortion are unconstitutional because they violate a woman's right to privacy.  

Workers, mostly women, at the Farah Pants factory walk out, call for boycott.  

International Women's Day--day of mourning solidarity with Vietnamese women.  

Margo St. James founds COYOTE, prostitutes' rights organization.  

Colorado Supreme Court finally gets around to declaring the state's abortion laws unconstitutional.  

I am a common woman,  
I have a common woman's head;  
And every common woman  

Is like a common loaf of bread.  

She will rise.  

Los Angeles Women's Liberation Union formed.  
Southwest Women's Festival, Tucson, AR.  
West Coast Lesbian Conference held in Los Angeles.  

United Farm Workers first grape contract expires; growers sign with the Teamsters in an attempt to kill the UFW. Head lettuce and Safeway still being boycotted, grapes are soon back on the list.  

Delegates from the Feminist Party Convention being held in Boston, led by Florence Kennedy, stage a “pee-in" at Harvard to protest pay toilets.  

Mother's Day--rallies held nationally in opposition to federal cutbacks in child care programs.  

International Feminist Planning Conference.  

The National Black Feminist Organization forms as a Black feminist action group addressing itself to the specific concerns of Black women.  

Denver Lesbian Center opens.  

The “Three Maria" go on trial in Portugal for writing a collection of feminist essays, stories, and poems which, according to censors, “outrage public morals."  

Denver Women's Coffeehouse opens (it's named "Mangas" which is Greek for the feminine form of the word for independence and self-sufficiency).  

Billy Jean King beats Bobby What's-his-name in a much-anticipated tennis match.  

Mountain States Women Abortion Coalition holds a “Crimes Against Women Tribunal" to present personal testimony of women who have had difficult experiences regarding abortions, birth control info, welfare and health departments, etc.

Workers win the right to unionize in a decision against Farah Manufacturing Co. Workers, (80% women) walked off their jobs almost 2 years ago in protest of the arbitrary firing of 6 organizers for the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America. The decision granted virtually all the relief sought by the workers.  

The First Women To Work On Big Mama Rag  

Women in Transition House celebrates its first anniversary. WIT House opened to serve as a drop-in center for crisis, conversation and sisterhood, offering emergency housing.  

Colorado Supreme Court rules the state abortion restrictions unconstitutional because of the residency requirement, that the abortion had to be performed in hospitals accredited by a private agency, applicants were screened by a hospital committee and a doctor had to certify the potential danger of a pregnancy.  

Women students take over the Colorado University Memorial Center in a protest for better day care.  

Midwest Lesbian Conference held in Michigan.  
Quest publishes its first issue, Quest provides a forum to develop political analysis and ideology and to provide a place for women to evaluate their experiences in the movement.  

The first National Women's Music Festival is held in Champaign, IL.  

The New Alexandria Library for Lesbian Women opens in Chicago.  
Denver's First Annual Women's Festival is held at East High.  

Joan Little is charged with the murder of a prison guard who tried to rape her.  

Lesbian Mother's National Defense Fund is started.  
Boulder Women's Resource Center opens.  

Creative Health Services opens in Denver to provide health care from a feminist point of view.  

Karen Silkwood dies after her car is forced off the road. Silkwood, a nuclear technician at the Kerr-McGee plant, was in the process of exposing health and safety violations and the falsification of quality control documents at the plant when she died.  

Inez Garcia is charged for murder when she kills one of two men who rape and beat her.  

A secret report is published that had been issued by the Governor of Puerto Rico's economic policy group entitled “Opportunities for Employment, Education and Training” which discusses as the main solution to unemployment massive sterilization of Puerto Rican women. Studies place the number of women of childbearing age who have been sterilized in Puerto Rico at 35%.  

Sisterhood is Powerful, Inc., a fund supported by royalties from the book Sisterhood is Powerful to give access to money for women's centers, health projects, newspapers, anti-rape groups, lesbian groups, welfare mothers, 3rd World Feminist groups, abortion campaigns, legal defense, etc., is sued for allegedly plagiarizing its bibliography.
