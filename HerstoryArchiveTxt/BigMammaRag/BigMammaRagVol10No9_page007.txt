Activist Murdered

Ruth First, a leading mili-
tant of the Afrikan National
Congress and a long-time acti-
vist in the struggle against
the US-backed Apartheid regime
in So. Africa, was killed by a
parcel bomb Aug. 17 in Maputo,
Mozambique. The bomb came in
an envelope mailed from Wash.
D.C. which bore the return ad-
dress of the African Bibliogra-
phy Center, distributed widely
to "Black intellectuals".

It is widely felt that the
So. African secret police
would never have dared to put
this bomb in an envelope sent
from Wash. D.C., under a group
name whose major source of
funding is the US government,
without the express permission
of the CIA. US Imperialism
supports and profits from So.
African apartheid. The evi-
dence around the First murder
indicates that the US also
actively participates in its

campaign to kill its opponents.

Pageant Protested

Two of twenty women in Peru
who were protesting the Miss
Universe pageant were arrest-
ed and brutally beaten as they
refused to get into a patrol
car. Leaflets distributed at
the protest pointed out that
"with beauty contests you don't
hide poverty," and "the money
spent in the contest should
have been invested in the ac-
quisition of equipment for the
maternity of Lima." The police
were not able to silence the
protests since all along the
course to the Plaza de Armas
a great number of leaflets
were thrown.

Sick Of Spray

Hampstead, UK Telephone Excha-

not allowed to leave the room.

at the time. A second woman
developed severe headaches
and blisters over her body.

Fraser Re-instated

Clara Fraser has insisted
for seven years that she was
fired in 1975 from her job as
Education Coordinator at Seat-
tle City Light for her sex and
political beliefs, wins her
job back plus $100,000! Manage-
ment had suddenly fired her,
under the guise of a budget
cut, after she became a leader
in an employee walkout, the
workers' representative on a
negotiating committee, and a par-
ticipant in the recall cam-
paign against Mayor Uhlman.
As a socialist feminist, she
has now won a decisive round
in her test case of Seattle's
Fair Employment Practices Or-
dinance, which forbids ideo-
logical discrimination.

Out of Control

In Seoul, South Korea, six
of the remaining trade unions
in the country were sacked by Control
Data, a U.S. multinational el-
ectronics plant. Control Data
is known for using lay-offs
and rehiring at reduced compen-
sation to reduce costs.

Strike action occurred, women
protesting salary discrimina-
tion and dangerous conditions
at the worksite. The strike
ended with a 19.5% wage incre-
ase. The women who were fired
are still trying to be reinstated,
the workers are conducting a
go-slow and an occupation of
some parts of the plant by off-
duty workers. The company has
responded by literally trying
to freeze them out by turning
up the air-conditioning.
