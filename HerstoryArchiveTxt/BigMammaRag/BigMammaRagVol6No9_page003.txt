contents  

Making the News 6-7  

The ERA Extension passed the House of Representatives by a  
whopping 44 votes. Two women who were there tell the story  
behind the headlines. Also, news about welfare women and a union  
conference.  

Are the Seven Sisters Obsolete? 8-9  

Single sex colleges have dwindled over the past ten years from 259  
to 193. The debate continues about whether women’s colleges are  
19th century leftovers or a newly-relevant alternative. Mary  
Maynard polled experts and students.  

A Class Act— Women’s Studies 11  

A short list of some of the women’s studies classes being offered  
around Boston this fall. The emphasis seems to be on “career/life  
planning” but the academic offerings have expanded in some  
interesting ways.  

A Dark Horse Pulls Ahead 12  

Only three seasons old, Dark Horse has a good chance of winning  
this year’s Mayor’s Cup of the Boston Parks and Recreation  
Department’s Women’s Softball League.  

Woman Abuse and the Law 12-13  

According to the FBI, wife abuse occurs every 18 seconds in this  
country. The problem isn’t new. The fact that women battering as a  
reality at all to our times. Laura Barrett explores the  

reasons women find it hard to leave abusive men.  

Happiness Isn’t Everything 14  

Final Payments by Mary Gordon is a particularly powerful book for  
women who grew up Catholic. But her novel about a woman  
“obsessed” is for everyone, “a deeply contemplative, enriching  
and delightful experience.”  

Mary Cassatt At Home 15  

Mary Cassatt was one of the two women Impressionists, and the  
only American whose work was exhibited with the French masters.  
Her lack of recognition in the States always disturbed her. But  
Cassat’s reputation has grown since her death.  

How to Buy a Stereo 16  

Buying sound equipment is an intimidating venture for most of us.  
Susan Wilson takes some of the anxiety out of the process with  
practical advice and questions to bring with you to the store. Also  
“Sound Advice” for September.  

Of Ducks and Perversity 17  

David Mamet’s two plays at the Charles Playhouse show the  
“enfant terrible” of Broadway at his best and worst. And the worst  
is pretty awful.
