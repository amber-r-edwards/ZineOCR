The International Conference On
Prison Abolition will be held
in Toronto, Canada, May 26-28.
The Conference is concerned with
establishing alternatives to
the present prison system and
will bring people together
from the US, Canada, and Europe.
Workshops will be organized
around the major topic head-
ings: Analyzing the System,
How to Get To Abolition From
Here, What To Do With The
Tough Cases/True Alternatives
to Prison. For registration
info write: International
Conference On Prison Aboli-
tion, 60 Lowther Avenue,
Toronto, Ontario M5R 1C7.

The 8th International Conference of
Gay and Lesbian Jews will be
held in Miami, Fla. August 4th
-7th, '83. The word "Miami"
when translated in Hebrew, be-
comes "Mi-Ami" and means "Who
Are My People?"--as Jews, as
Lesbians and Gays, and as re-
sponsible adults. This fortun-
ate coincidence provides the
theme for this year's conference. For
registration info write: 8th
International Conference Committee, 19094
West Dixie Highway, North Mi-
ami Beach, Fla. 33180.

The 5th National Lesbian/Gay
Health Conference will be held in
Denver, CO, June 9--12. The
Conference includes the Second Nat.
Forum on Acquired Immune Defi-
ciency Syndrome (AIDS), the
annual meeting of the American Association
of Physicians for Human Rights.
Some of the themes to be ad-
dressed at the conference include:
Racism in the Lesbian and Gay
Community, Health and Politics,
Artificial Insemination, Al-
coholism and Incest, Alcohol
and Chemical Dependency in the
Lesbian/Gay Community. For registra-
tion info write: Health Pio-
neering in the 80's, P.O. Box
9413, Denver, CO 80209, or
call (415) 653-3724 in CA,
(303) 321-1737 in CO.

The 1st National Conference of Wo-
men in the Trades will take
place May 28-30th, in the SF
Bay area. Workshops will focus
on four major groups: union
women, self-employed trades-
women, entry-level women, and
advocates for tradeswomen. At-
tention will be paid to issues
unique to Third World women.
For more info send a legal-
sized SASE to: Tradeswomen,
Inc., Box 40664, SF, CA 94140,
or call Tradeswomen Inc. at
(415) 989-1566 or Dorian King
(415) 834-8268 (Women of Co-
lor/Third World women).
