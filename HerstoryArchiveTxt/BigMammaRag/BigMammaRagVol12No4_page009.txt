Marie Rose not sur-

Marie Rose was a principal
of a school for retarded
children. But in the novel,
she teaches deaf-mutes,
because, Adnan explains,
"I consider that the deaf
mutes represent the Arab
people."

A Lebanese poet, painter
and tapestry designer,
Etel Adnan, 58, is the
daughter of a Greek
Christian mother and Syr-
ian Muslim father. Educa-
ted in French and American
schools, she taught phil-
osophy for 14 years in
California, where she
now lives. She has pub-
lished 5 books of poetry.

It was during her stu-
dent days at Berkeley and
Harvard that she reclaimed
her Arab identity, and in
the early '60s she
helped found an education-
al organization called
Najda: Women Concerned
About the Middle East.

Adnan describes the act
of writing as a positive
gesture...I think when
you write the saddest
things, while you're
writing them, they seem
more hopeful."

At times you'll feel
hopeful while reading
Sitt Marie Rose; at other
times you'll cry and de-
spair. But at all times
her writing will touch
your heart and mind.
