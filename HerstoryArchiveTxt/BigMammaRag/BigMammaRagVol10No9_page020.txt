The National Women's Health Network is sponsoring the BLACK WOMEN'S HEALTH CONFERENCE June 24-26, 1983 at Spelman College in Atlanta. Issues to be addressed at the conference include: high blood pressure, heart disease, diabetes, teen pregnancy, stress, sexism, racism, domestic violence, abortion, rape and many more. The network has established a speakers bureau with a slide show presentation which outlines demographic information, chronic diseases, information about the Nat'l Women's Health Network, and our educational program. If you want to be on the mailing list, or set up a presentation in your area, or get more conference info, write Byllye Avery, Black Women's Health Project, 224 Seventh St. S.E. Washington, DC 20003 (202) 543-9222.

The First National Lesbians of Color Conference in Southern CA is being planned for the 3-day weekend of July 4, 1983. The planning committee needs input now. If you have a topic, workshop idea, etc., please consider volunteering to accomplish it. If you have a mailing list of Third World lesbians and/or organizations please share it with us. We are especially concerned about being able to reach our Asian-Pacific and Native American lesbian sisters, as well as achieving a genuinely national conference. For more info and to give your input write: Los Angeles Lesbians of Color, PO Box 2344, Los Angeles, CA 90051.

Sister To Sister, the Second Annual Black Lesbian Conference will be held October 8, 9, and 10, 1982. I.S. 136 Manhattan, 6 Edgecomb Ave at 135th St, NYC. The organizers, the Committee for the Visibility of the Other Black Women: the Black Lesbian hope to provide the women of the Black community an opportunity to meet and share in a learning and cultural experience, and establish a basis for future communication and provide a support network within our community. To register, write CVOBW c/o Johnson 72-15 41st Ave., Jackson Heights, NY 11337. Or call (212) 860-4936 Grey, (212) 565-8365 Johnson, or (212) 982-6280 Pinson.

Conference on Career Opportunities for Visually Impaired Women will be held at LaGuardia Community College on Nov. 6. It is a free conference on employment opportunities for blind and visually impaired women. It is designed to help high school and college students and working women find and reach their vocational goals. Guides will be available around the college and from the subway. For more info, call Robert Jones at (516) 626-2705. Registration deadline Oct. 15.

ANTI-SEXIST STRATEGIES and ANTI-RACIST STRATEGIES are sound-color filmstrips developed by the Council on Interracial Books for Children. These presentations help children become aware of the various forms of sexism and racism; both are useful for teacher training, staff and parent meetings, daycare centers, and elementary school classes. For more info contact the CIBC, 1841 Broadway, New York, NY 10023. (212) 757-5339.

looking for

Countervision, a photojournal on cultural diversity in the community of women, is announcing a call for entries from women photographers. The deadline for submissions is Nov. 30, 1982. A maximum of 10 unmounted, 8x10, black & white glossies will be accepted from each photographer along with an entry fee of $5. Please include SASE. For a more in-depth prospectus, write: Countervision, c/o Diana Duff, 3021 Rawson St. Oakland CA 94619 or call (415) 436-8030 leaving your name and address.

The National Festival of Women's Theatre at Santa Cruz, CA (May 16-22, 1983) requires a letter of availability for booking by Oct. 15, 1982. Women's performance groups, women workshop leaders, solo performers and lecturers are invited to respond. Works addressing the media image of women in America are particularly encouraged. For a free copy of our recent newsletter and our booking info form write: BOOKING: National Festival of Women's Theatre, P.O. Box 1222, Santa Cruz, CA 95061.

Out From Under: Sober Dykes and Our Friends is a new anthology on the stories and dreams of those of us involved with recovery from alcoholism or drug addiction. The editor has announced a call for material from women who are making clean and sober lives in lesbian communities as well as in the communities of our birth cultures and classes. It is a call for material from any woman who has become a clean or sober dyke, or who loves clean and sober dykes, or who wants to live in a community where there is strength and joy and the beginning of an end to victimization in lesbian lives. Articles and artwork welcome. Send SASE for more details. Deadline: Jan 1, 1983. Send to 11 Broderick, #5, San Francisco, CA, 94117.
