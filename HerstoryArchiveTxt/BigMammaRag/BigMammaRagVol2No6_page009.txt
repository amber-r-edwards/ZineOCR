American Association of
University Women (AAUW)

AAUW is a nationwide
organization with 31 local
branches in Colorado (seven in
metropolitan area). Major
concerns are study-action groups
on women, media, learning and
foreign policy, community
involvement, implementation of
AAUW — federal and state
legislative programs,

improvement of education at all
levels, and financial support for
fellowships and research and
project grants for women.
Membership is open to all
women who have bachelor’s or
higher degree from a college or
university. For information,

contact Colleen Lautenbach,
5088 Eldridge, Golden 80401.

Better Jobs for Women

Women, do you want to be a
carpenter, plumber or heavy
equipment operator? If you are
a woman living in Denver and
state this type of occupational
direction to a Better Jobs for
Women staff member, you won't
receive the usual pat on the head
with a dampening remark
“Little girls don’t plumb, dear!’’

BJW, funded by a Department
of Labor contract, is an out-
reach program designed to place
women into skilled trade and
craft-registered apprenticeship
or similar on-job training
programs. All BJW placements
are into full-time, full-phase
training programs, averaging in
length of three to five year time
periods. BJW makes no
part-time or temporary
placements. If seeking entry into
the skilled trades, call BJW for
an appointment at 244-4189.

Boulder Valley Clinic

This is a private, non-profit
abortion clinic which is open to
any woman considering an
ambulatory abortion (patient
under 18 must have parental
consent). The purpose of the
BVC is to provide a humane,
supportive, dignified atmosphere
for women seeking a
first-trimester abortion.
Emphasis is on counselling which
helps an undecided patient review
the alternatives for a problem
pregnancy. The fee of $110
442-5160.

second dealing with “Our Bodies

program for gay women is
starting in early March. For
information about these services
and their cost contact CHS,
4645 E. Colfax, 321-0567.

Mommie and Me

This is a program designed for
mother and child to enjoy gym
activities or swimming together.
In the first half of the morning
the emphasis is on physical
activities while the remaining
time is scheduled for discussion
on topics of concern to mothers.
Also during this time the
children are involved in art,
music, storytelling, and creative
dramatics. There are sessions on
Mondays and Wednesdays from
9:30-11:45 a.m. (the next
session begins on March 18-10).
For more information and
registration call Ceci Wells
(986-3797) or Jean Hoffman
(922-3295).

Re-engagement Program

To help meet the needs of many
women who have been unable to
obtain a college degree at the
traditional college age, Colorado
Women’s College has initiated
the Re-Engagement Program.
This program is geared to the
woman over 25 who wants an
opportunity to return to formal
degree study or to attend college
for the first time. A woman can
elect to carry an academic load
which meets her working and/or
family obligations. There is also
a possibility for non-graded
classes and a Child Care Center is
also available. For more
information contact Ms. Nancy
Scott at CWC, 394-6867.
