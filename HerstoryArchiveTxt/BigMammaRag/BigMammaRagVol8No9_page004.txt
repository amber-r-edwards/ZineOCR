After winning a substantial decision in the Appellate Court, on July 30, 1980, the Illinois Attorney General appealed the case against the Association for Childbirth at Home, International to the Illinois Supreme Court. In the lengthy decision by the Illinois District 4 Appellate Court, Judges Green, Mills, and Lewis found:

1) That based on the 1958 Supreme Court ruling of Alabama vs. NAACP, the State could not require the Association For Childbirth At Home, International (ACHI) to submit the names of its membership or names of physicians and midwives doing home births;

2) That ACHI's right to teach medical information was covered by the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution;

3) That the list of items subpoenaed from ACHI by the Illinois Attorney General's office had no bearing on ACHI's right to certify childbirth educators whether certification constituted consumer fraud;

4) That an organization engaged in education was not covered by the Illinois Consumer Fraud Act.

Judge Green stated, "We share defendants' concern with the scope of investigative power given by the Act (to the Attorney General)...We are convinced that the complaint shows on its face that the defendants are engaged involving an exercise of rights protected by the First Amendment...We conclude that ordering discovery which could be directed against such conduct (certifying childbirth educators and teaching parents home birth classes) would be violative of defendants' constitutional rights."
