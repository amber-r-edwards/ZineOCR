COMMON DIFFERENCES: THIRD
WORLD WOMEN AND FEMINIST PER-
SPECTIVES CONFERENCE will be
held at the Univ. of Ill. at
Urbana-Champaign, April 9-13,
1983. Our main goals are to
provide a forum where issues
pertaining to third world wo-
men can be articulated in re-
lation to the recent develop-
ments in feminist theory and
to encourage ongoing dialogue
and criticisms between "third
world” and “first world" wo-
men. Panels, workshops and
roundtable discussions will
be organized around the fol-
lowing: Colonization and Re-
sistance (April 10), Third
World Women: Images and Reali-
ties, International Women's
Movements (April 12). Partici-
pants at the Conf.: Patricia
Bell Scott-US, Yayori Matsui-
Japan, Christine Choy-US, He-
leith Saffioti-Brazil, Brenda
Verner-US, Irene Mack-US, Ra-
ya Dunayevskaya-USSR, Nellie
Wong-US, Virginia Vargas-Peru,
Sarah Maidoror-France, Cher-
rie Moraga-US, Julia Lesage-
US, Nawal El Saadawi-Egypt,
Madhu Sen-India.

For more info, contact Chan-
dra Talpade Mohanty, and Ann
Russo, Conference Coordinators;
Office for Women's Re-
sources and Services, 346 Fred
H. Turner Student Services Bldg, 610
East John Street, Champaign,
Illinois 61820, (217) 333-3137.

film or demonstration at the

the idea to Program Committees,
National Association of Lesbian

Suite 8, San Francisco, CA 94109.

The Elizabeth Stone House is
a program for women who are
in emotional stress and their
children. It combines low-
cost residential services
with advocacy and support for
resolving practical and emo-
tional problems. It is run by
and for women, it is afforda-
ble to poor women, children
stay with their mothers, and
the residents retain respon-
sibility for themselves at
all times. Write them for fur-
ther information: PO Box 15,
Jamaica Plain, Massachusetts
(617) 522-3417.
