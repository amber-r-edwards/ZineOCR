The obvious assault against
women must be directly con-
fronted by women. Grand Jury
activity has been used against
the women's community in the
past. In early 1975 grand ju-
ries were convened in Lexing-
ton, Ken. and Hartford, Conn.
ostensibly for the purpose of
investigating connections be-
tween two fugitives wanted in
connection with the 1970 "rob-
bery" of a Boston Bank, Susan
Saxe and Katherine Powers,
and various women's communi-
ties. These grand juries fo-
cused on Susan Saxe's lesbian-
ism as a pretext for interro-
gating a wide range of femi-
nist and lesbian groups in
the area and basically con-
ducting a "fishing expedition"
aimed at the women's movement.
Six activists from the lesbian
and gay liberation movement
refused to cooperate and were
jailed in Lexington. Two wo-
men, feminist activists, were
jailed in connection with the
Hartford Grand Jury. The
