BARGAINING FOR EQUALITY is  
a guide to legal and collec-  
tive bargaining solutions  
for workplace problems that  
particularly affect women.  
Bargaining for Equality is  
an excellent resource for:  
evaluating workplace problems,  
learning legal rights, stim-  
ulating union organizing,  
formulating collective bar-  
gaining demands, increasing  
women's participation in  
unions. Send $4.50 each plus  
$1.50 (first class) or 50¢  
(book rate) postage and hand-  
ling to Women's Labor Project,  
PO Box 6250, San Francisco,  
CA 94101.

INCEST: A RESOURCE MANUAL is  

a 25-page booklet produced by  
the Rape Crisis Centre in Tor-  
onto. It offers an in-depth  
feminist perspective on incest  
and an annotated bibliography  
of incest materials. The book-  
let goes far in placing incest  
in the social context of vio-  
lence against women. Copies  
available free of charge from  
the Toronto Rape Crisis Centre,  
PO Box 6597, Station A, Toron-  
to, Ontario M5W 1X4.

WE ALL HAVE OUR REASONS may  
be the first film to explore  
the reality of drinking and  
women. Filmed on location  
with interviews in women's  
bars, along the streets, and  
at the Alcoholism Center for  
Women in Los Angeles, the  
film explores the impact of  
this drug in women's lives.  
We All Have Our Reasons was  
produced by Iris Films for  
the Alcoholism Center for  
Women. For more info write  
the Center, 1147 Alvarado St.,  
Los Angeles, CA 90006.

IN OUR OWN HOMES is a slide-  
tape show that deals with the  
ways in which older people in  
Canada have been separated  
from the mainstream of life  
through institutionalization  
and/or isolation. It touches  
on a variety of areas where  
change is needed. It's avail-  
able from Development Educa-  
tion in Action, 250 Heath St.  
W., Toronto, Ontario M5P 3L4.  
Rental cost is $10-$20.

NEW DIRECTIONS FOR YOUNG  
WOMEN is unique for its focus  
on young women in conflict  
with the law. The project  
intends to redress the un-  
equal and detrimental treat-  
ment young women receive in  
the courts, schools, and so-  
cial service systems. The  
project maintains staffed  
offices in Tucson, Arizona  
and Salem, Oregon. Additional  
info, as well as a list of  
project publications, may be  
had by writing to National  
Female Advocacy Project, 376  
S. Stone Ave., Tucson, AZ  
85701.

THE COMMUNITY FUND is getting  
low on funds. Since November,  
the Community Fund has given

needing food, rent, medical  
bills and other basic surviv-

keep going if it receives  
financial support from those  
who have a little (or a lot)  
to spare. Donations can be  
sent to the Community Fund,  
PO Box 7221, Olympia, WA  
98507.
