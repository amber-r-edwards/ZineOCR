Kitchen Table: Women of Color  
Press is committed to produ-  
cing and distributing the work  
of Third World women of all  
racial/cultural heritages,  
sexualities and classes that  
will further the cause of  
Third World women's personal  
and political freedom. They  
welcome contributions, finan-  
cial or material, such as  
mailing lists, etc., and as  

of Jan. 1 they will be happy  
to look at outlines for book  
projects—please don't send  
completed manuscripts. "We  
will only publish women of  
color, with good (intentioned)  
hearts and strong minds."

Any woman of color in the  

NY area who is interested in  
helping make this press work  
get in touch with them. P.O.  
Box 592, Van Brunt Sta.,  
Brooklyn, NY 11215.

Isis International Bulletins,  
an International Women's In-  
formation and Communication  
Service is setting up an ex-  
change program for feminist  
projects around the world.  
The program will provide be-  
tween 6 and 10 one-year in-  
ternships for women to work  
and learn in another cultural  
context. The exchanges will  
be between industrialized  
countries (IC) and developing  
countries (DC), between IC  
and IC and between DC and DC.  
Projects must be able to pro-  
vide an intern with a living  
allowance, health and dental  
insurance and round trip eco-  
nomy air fare. If interested  
write to Isis by Feb. 15th,  
1982 at P.O. Box 50 (Corna-  
vin), 1211 Geneva 2, Switzer-  
land.
