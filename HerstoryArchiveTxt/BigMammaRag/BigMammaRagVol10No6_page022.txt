Grandma Was an Activist is a
series of radio programs that
explores the role of rank and
file women radicals in the
social and political turbu-
lence of the 30's through
interviews, sound and narra-
tion. Includes the voices of
women who recall the Renais-
sance and riots of Harlem and
beyond, tenant organizers,
and many others against the
backdrop which includes the
Great Depression, the rise of
Fascism, emergence of Stalin-
ism, and finally the outbreak

of war. For more info contact:
Charles Potter, Radio Arts
Inc., 838 West End Ave (6-D)
New York, New York 10025.

Antelope Publications is a
new feminist publishing house
located in Denver, CO, whose
purpose is to publish current
feminist political ideas and
to disseminate them as quick-
ly and inexpensively as pos-
sible. "We want to revive the
art of political pamphleteer-
ing by printing edited ver-
sions of contemporary speech-
es, workshops, and lectures.
They are interested in tapes
or transcripts of speeches

or workshops by feminists,
please contact them if you
know of any they might be in-
terested in. Their first one
is "Feminism in the '80s:
Facing Down the Right" by
Charlotte Bunch, for $3. Con-
tact them at 1612 St. Paul,
Denver, CO 80206.

The Triple Struggle is a new
book about Latin American
peasant women. Women in Boli-
via, Ecuador, El Salvador,
Guatemala and Peru express

in their own words the triple
struggle. It is a fight
against the oppression of
imperialism, the poverty of
the peasant class and the
position of women in a male-
dominated society. For £3
from WOW Campaigns Ltd, 467a
Caledonian Rd., London,

The Midwest Amazon Mothers
Alliance (MAMA) has created
a survey that is by, for and
about lesbian mothers and
their families. The survey's
purpose is to compile system-
atically obtained information
about lesbian mothers, their
lovers/partners, and their
children to help fight court-
room prejudices in custody
cases, and the ignorance in
local communities. To parti-
cipate send a self-addressed
envelope with 2 stamps to
MAMA, c/o Toman, P.O. Box 268,
DeKalb, IL 60115. All replies
will be confidential.
