Summer School  

Registration for Woman to Woman  
Feminist School is continuing through  
the first week of July, and classes  
begin the week of July 4. Fees are  
on a sliding scale from $1 to $10, ex-  
cept for free events noted below.  
Register at Woman to Woman, 2023 E.  
Colfax, Mon. through Sat., 10 a.m. to 8  
p.m. Here's what you can choose from:  

For those who want an active sum-  
mer, try volleyball (6:30 p.m. Mondays),  
rugby (10 a.m. Sundays) or hiking and  
backpacking. The latter is a workshop  
the second week of July followed by  
two day hikes and a weekend outing.  
Class limit 8.  

For those who want to spend at least  
part of the summer sitting down, you  
might consider:  

. The "coming out" group. Meets  
Thursdays, 8 p.m. at Woman to Woman  
beginning July 7. Talk about the ram-  
ifications of wearing a dyke T-shirt  
to work, the Denver dyke scene, par-  
ents, the endless monogamy question  
and other fun topics. Free.  

. General CR group. Wednesdays at  
8 p.m. at Woman to Woman. Begins July 6,  
and runs 8 weeks. Free.  

. Study group. Meets Mondays at  
8 p.m. at Woman to Woman beginning July  
11. Catch up on politics, herstory or  
whatever. Free.  

. Feminism and Socialism meets  
Tuesdays at 7 p.m. Facilitator: Andrea  
Gabriel. Focus on the strengths and  
weaknesses of working class perspec-  
tives in theory and practice.  

. Lesbo lit by Tea Schook meets  
Wednesdays at 6 p.m. Recapture great  
moments of lesbian folk-herstory.  

. Work on your car and learn how  
to do a tune-up Wednesdays at 6 p.m.  
Debbie Squires teaches. Limit 15.  

. Folk dance workshop, Saturday,  
July 9, 7 p.m. at Cheesman Park. Inter-  
national non-sexist folk dances. By  
Clair and Clare.  

. Day care/child care workshop fo-  
cuses on getting good child care you  
can afford. Facilitated by Phyllis  
Kadison and two women from the day  
care co-op. Saturday, Aug. 20, 1-4 p.m.  

. Racism workshop, Saturday, July  
23, 1-4 p.m. (See calendar).
