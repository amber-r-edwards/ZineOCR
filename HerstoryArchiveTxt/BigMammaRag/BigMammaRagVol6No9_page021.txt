CULINARY INSTRUCTOR

General Statement of Duties: Instruct and train CETA  
participants to a level of competence which will qualify them  
for entry level culinary positions in the private sector.  
Supervision Received: Works under supervision of Manager  
of Training Center.

Supervision Given: Supervise classroom aide and students.  
Examples of Duties:

Including but not limited to:

1. Develop a curriculum which will cover all the necessary  
skills. (Length of course is 6 months).

2. Provide full time instruction (7 hr. day)

3. Assist in determining equipment needs.

4. Order all necessary accessories and supplies.

5. Maintain all equipment according to manufacturer's  
recommendations.

6. Determine course entry level requirements.

7. Assist in interviewing clients.

8. Maintain contact with private sector to ensure entry level  
culinary skills are being taught.

9. Notify the proper personnel of any disciplinary problems.  
10. Assist in job placement of students.

11. Maintain progress records on each student.

12. Determine students' supportive educational needs in  
conjunction with supportive education staff.

13. Provide for maintenance of kitchen and dining area by  
assigning students to clean areas.

General Requirements:

1. At least 6 years experience as a chef.

2. Familiarity with Hotel and Restaurant cooking require-  
ments.

3. Certification or potential to be certified.*

Salary: $10,985 and up, depending on experience.

*Certification Requirements:  
a. 6 years recent, full time experience in the trade.  
b. File with Division of Occupational Education to take  
competency exams when next given.  
c. 18 credits in Teacher Training within 3 years after  
passing exam.  
Qualified applicants should submit a resume and a letter of  
application by 9/5/78 to:

Mr. Edward Hannon
