THE COLORADO CITIZENS CAUCUS, with  
renewed interest and energy, is in the  
developmental stages of organizing a  
statewide lobby which will address  
issues of concern to lesbians and gays.  
The primary purpose of the caucus is  
to ward off oppressive measures and  
encourage positive measures in the  
Colorado legislature. Updating the  
lesbian/gay community statewide of  
pending legislation by the state law-  
making body will also be one of its  
functions. Correspondence should be  
addressed to the Colorado Citizens  
Caucus, P.O. Box 18701, Denver, CO  
80218.

ANNOUNCING: A WOMEN'S CHAPBOOK Con-  
test. Open to all WOMEN POETS residing  
in the U.S. who have not yet publish-  
ed a chapbook. Entry fee is $5, inclu-  
des one copy of the annual anthology of  
California Women Poets. The winning  
chapbook writer will receive $25  

and 100 copies of her work. 25 copies  
will be retained by WTWL to be dona-  
ted as gifts, prizes and contribu-  
tions to women centers. Guidelines:  
Each typed manuscript should contain  
25 numbered pages of poetry by one  
author plus a table of contents.  

For previously published poems, the  
entrant is responsible for getting  
permission and crediting, a Title  
page, a brief bio and a second copy  
of the contents page should be sub-  
mitted detached from the manuscript.  
Author's name should not appear on  
the manuscript except for the detach-  
ed bio and title page, so that the  
submissions can be judged without any  
identification. A typed 3X5 card  
should be included listing name, ad-  
dress, zip, phone and title of manu-  
script. SASE must be included, more  
than one manuscript may be submitted.

AN ALL WOMEN'S CANOE TRIP TO THE  
Canadian boundary waters during the  
last two weeks of August 1980 is now  
in the beginning planning stages.  
Women of all ages and experience are  
welcome. This canoe trip is sponsored  
by the Women's Outdoor Club in Denver,  
CO. Food, equipment and transportation  
expenses as well as skills will be  
shared. Collectively the group will  
need the following skills: canoeing,  
portaging, first aid, life saving,  
CPR, camping and navigating. Basic  

swimming skills are needed individually.  

The first organizational meeting is on  
Tuesday, May 13, 7:30 P.M. at the  
Gay Community Center in Denver.
