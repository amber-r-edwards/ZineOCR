conferences

THE OZARK WOMYN'S TRUCKING
COLLECTIVE will host a work-
shop entitled "Womyn in Col-
lectives" on Oct. 9-11. The
womyn in the Ozark collective
have long felt isolated being
the only womyn's collective
in the area. They want to
reach out to other womyn in
collective working situations
in order to share experiences,
frustrations, struggles and
joy. All women working in
collectives, including womyn
working with men, are encour-
aged to come. Some ideas for
workshops are: Power and Hier-
archy, Working with Womyn of
Color in Collective Situ-
ations, and how to have anoth-
er life. Cost is $10-15.
Housing, meals and childcare
provided. The weekend will be
held at the Community Pre-
School in Fayetteville, AK.
Leave pets home.

WOMEN AND MULTINATIONALS, wo-
men in Northern Ontario have
organized a conference con-
cerning the effects on women of
multinationals in Canada. Sing-
le industry towns, the food in-
dustry and the pharmaceutical
industry will be examined. Kari
Levitt, a professor of econom-
ics at McGill U. and author of
Silent Surrender, and Rhonda
Love, a professor of Community
Health at the U. of Toronto
will be among the speakers.

Due to financial restrictions
the conference is open to NE
Ontario women only; other women
by invite only. Oct. 16-18 in
Sault Ste. Marie. Contact Joan
Kuyek, 232 Lansdowne St., Sud-
bury, Ontario P3C 4M3.
