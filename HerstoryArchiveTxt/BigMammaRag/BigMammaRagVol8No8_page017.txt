Women in prison may receive a free  
subscription to the publication  
PRISONS, dealing with issues of

THE BLATANT IMAGE is a new femin-  
ist photography magazine that is  
planning its first publication in June,  
1981. They are seeking practical,  
personal, aesthetic, critical, as  
well as technical articles dealing  
with feminist photography, together  
with photographs to illustrate them.  
Women photographers are invited to  
send a favorite photograph of theirs  
along with about 250 words telling  
how it felt to take the photo, how  
or why it was taken, what the photo  
means to her, and other relevant  
ideas. In addition, up to 5 photos  
no larger than 8x10 in black and white,  
which a woman photographer considers  
to be her best, most interesting, or  
most experimental work may be sent.  
The address is Box 56, Wolf Creek,  
OR 97497. Materials will be accept-  
ed until March 1, 1981. Copies of  
the first limited edition of the  
magazine may be reserved for $10  
sent to the same address. For in-  
formation on tax deductible gifts  
to support the project, write to  
Moonforce Media, P.O. Box 2934,  
Washington, D.C. 20013.

WORKING FOR YOUR LIFE is an hour-  
length color film that looks at the  
hazards faced by working women. It  
is the only film about the health  
and safety of women on the job. It  
rents for $65 ($85 for two shows  
within two days) and sells for $475.  
For information, write LOHP Films,  
P.O. Box 315, Franklin Lakes, N.J.  
07417, phone (201) 891-8240.

CLEIS PRESS is a new feminist  
publishing company whose first books  
will appear in 1981. They are  
researching a large resource book  
documenting the feminist resistance  
movement against male violence,  
and would like personal narratives  
by women who have fought back in  
various ways against violence and  
pornography. They are also look-  
ing for articles of feminist theory  
on tactics for fighting back and  
analysis. They are also compiling  
a directory of organizations and  
individuals working in this strug-  
gle—especially women who teach  
Karate and self-defense. Articles,  
stories, essays, letters, photo-  
graphs, and artwork (do not send  
originals) can be sent to 3141  
Pleasant Avenue, South, Minneapolis,  
Minnesota 55408. Please include a  
SASE. Deadline is October 1, 1980.

The COALITION OF GRASS ROOTS WO-  
MEN is a new national organization  
"to fight for the needs of the maj-  
ority of women in this country who  
are working women and poor women."  
Their press release claims that they  
are "the beginning of a new women's  
movement" (quite a claim!). One  
of their aims is "to support the  
building of independent electoral  
political parties in opposition to  
the currently established parties."  
The organization came out of the  
1980 Women's Convention which was  
held in NYC, June 22. If you're in-  
terested, the address is 1133 Broad-  
way, Room 517, New York, NY 10010,  
phone (212) 243-7300.

culture

The WALLFLOWER ORDER is a women's  
dance collective from Eugene, Ore-  
gon, that has been together for  
five years. Their main emphasis  
is dance, but they also incorporate  
theatre and original music into  
their work. Their content explores  
women's issues, women in prison,  
gay and lesbian rights, and "the  
myriad of thoughts and emotions of  
the women in this country."

They are going to be on tour this  
fall and winter, appearing every-  
where from Vancouver, B.C. to  
Washington, D.C. (but, alas, not in  
Denver!). Be sure to watch for  
them.

The Feminist Writers' Guild has  
announced its FIRST ANNUAL WOMEN OF  
PROMISE AWARD for previously un-  
published women writers, in conjunc-  
tion with its New York chapter.

The award will be sponsored yearly  
for different literary forms and  
will be administered by different  
Guild chapters on a rotating basis.  
The 1980-81 award will be in poetry  
and will be administered by the New  
York Chapter. All Guild members  
will be eligible to become candi-  
dates for the award. A three-mem-  
ber panel will choose the winners.  
First, second and third place poets  
will be listed in the Guild's nat-  
ional newsletter and published in a  
women's small press magazine or  
journal. Manuscripts may be sub-  
mitted until November 15, 1980.  
Winners will be selected January 15,  
1981. For more information on  
qualifications for entry or on the  
Guild, write the Feminist Writers'  
Guild, P.O. Box 9396, Berkeley, CA  
94709.

10 admitted. Childcare will be  
available and it is wheelchair ac-  
cessible. For information and tic-  
kets write to: West Coast Women's  
Music Festival, 1197 Valencia, San  
Francisco, CA 94110, phone (415)  
641-4892.
