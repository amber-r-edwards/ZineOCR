AUGUST 26:  
EQUALITY DAY AROUND THE NATION  

Mary Lynn Myers, (left) NOW Finance VP, leads parade in Brookings, SD to celebrate Women’s Equality Day. Myers, Director of Human Rights for South Dakota, addressed an evening gathering on job discrimination.  

Women throughout the nation gathered on August 26 to celebrate the 55th anniversary of the ratification of the 19th Amendment giving women the right to vote. From newsletters, clippings, and press releases, these are some of the highlights you have furnished describing local chapter celebrations of Women's Equality Day. Read how feminists in many areas congregated and spoke out for the Equal Rights Amendment, full employment, equal justice, and spoke out against all forms of violence against women under our banner, “Stop Violence Against Women NOW.”  

* Ventura, CA Department of Defense sponsored Women’s Awareness Week featuring men and women speaking on topics of women’s concerns. Tish Sommers, NOW's Task Force Coordinator on Older Women, talked about women as displaced homemakers. The whole week was structured to involve men and women in the county, making them aware of women's concerns.  

* Bakersfield, CA NOW designated a “Week of Action to Stop Crimes of Violence Against Women.” There were programs focusing on rape and self-defense.  

* Seattle, WA NOW members walked an informational picket line outside local EEOC offices protesting violation of state and federal maternity leave guidelines by five area employers. NOW's employment task force presented EEOC with formal complaints against the local employers.  

* Aberdeen, SD women met to hear NOW Vice President and State Director of Human Rights Mary Lynn Myers tell her audience, “The day has passed, if indeed there ever was a day, when most women worked for extra spending money or to occupy their leisure time. Women work out of necessity.”  

* Hastings, NE NOW held workshops on volunteerism, men’s participation in the feminist movement, monitoring the new Nebraska sexual assault law, how to find a job, older women, and legislation that affects women.  

* Memphis, TN NOW sponsored a night march through the streets of Memphis protesting violence against women.  

* Oklahoma City, OK NOW participated in a festival celebrating International Women’s Year with displays and booths highlighting the projects and goals of the participating organizations. The festival featured entertainment, displays of work by artists and crafts-women, and an International fashion show.  

* Window Rock, NW NOW members donated a selection of books about women to the Window Rock Public Library to increase the community’s awareness of women and their concerns.  

* Houston, TX NOW celebrated with a party open to the public. Donations were accepted and proceeds will go toward running the Women's Center.
