N. O.W. CHECK LIST FOR ACTION: FALL 1973:

Get the Mail Out — Educate Congress.  
Get phone calls to home offices of Senators and Congresspersons.  
Make appointments to see Senators and Congresspersons at Thanksgiving Recess time.  

Work on PAR Congressional Research kits.  
THANKSGIVING 1973  

Meet with your Senators and Congresspersons:  

Ask them their stand on the Constitutional Amendments.  

Inform them of NOW’s position.  

Ask them if they answered NOW questionnaire on Supreme Court decision.  
Report results of interviews to NOW Legislative Office.  

DECEMBER 1973  
Finish up PAR Congressional Research kit. Mail results in to NOW Legislative office.  
JANUARY 1974  

Have letters to Senators and Congresspersons waiting on their desks by January 15, 1974  
when they return to session.  

Remind them in the letters about the questionnaire and about their statements at your  
Thanksgiving interview.  
FEBRUARY — MARCH 1974  

Primary watches — The opposition has claimed they will challenge pro-Supreme Court  
decision Senators and Congresspersons in the primaries. The Legislative office will need  
solid information on where abortion becomes a hot primary issue and the results.  

LEGAL ARGUMENTS AGAINST CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT:  

(Because feminist arguments against the proposed constitutional amendment to prohibit  
abortion are often ineffective, it is important that we learn to use a broad range of reasons  
for opposition. The following are some suggestions which you might find helpful; learn  
to use them!)  

1. CONSTITUTIONAL PURITY  

Don’t junk up the Constitution — technology could make the abortion issue obsolete —  
opens the door to busing and school prayer amendments — establishes an absolute in  
a fluid, pluralistic society.
